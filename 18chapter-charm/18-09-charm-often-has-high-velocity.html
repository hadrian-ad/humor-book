The Humor Book

Charm often has high-velocity

Charm often involves high-velocity movement toward humor outcomes—since in complicated situations, low-velocity comments don’t always reach humor outcomes:

Tutor: *finishes reading Zoe’s grad school application essay* *sighs* “This is pretty terrible.” (←tutor rushes toward outcomes like controlling the situation, opening new topics of conversation, acceptably criticizing, and so on)

Zoe: *cathartic laugh* (←tutor rushes Zoe into humor outcome tripwires) “Really? Oh no.”

- OR -

Tutor: *finishes reading Zoe’s grad school application essay* “Um. So this is… okay. There were things I liked about it. But we should maybe talk about your approach.” (←tutor goes gently toward humor outcomes like controlling the situation, opening new topics of conversation, acceptably criticizing, and so on)

Zoe: *confused* “So I’m on the right track?” (←Zoe doesn’t reach any humor outcomes)

If the tutor used less velocity, Zoe may have defended against those tripwires and not have felt humor with as much fittedness. In this case, the “nicer” version would be worse charm!
