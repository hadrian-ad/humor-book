The Humor Book

We tolerate what seems justified

When something is well-fitted, we can’t effectively censure it. Since everyone sees the humor attempt as justified, they'll find our censure unpersuasive:

We sometimes feel this with fables, proverbs, satire, and so on which are seen as above justified reproach:

At a department meeting, allegations of misconduct by an adjunct professor are brought up.

Justin: "He's always had some quirks. But how much should we act no these allegations?"

Dave: "Where there's smoke, there's fire."

The department considers this.

Jessica is upset at an implication Emma makes about Elias.

Emma: (cautiously) "It's just like, you know, the Boy Who Cried Wolf."

Jessica is still upset, but doesn't get more upset at the reference. It sticks in her head.

Satire website headline: “Nation’s 10-Year-Old Boys: ‘If You See Someone Raping Us, Please Call the Police’ (‘Doesn’t Matter Who, Doesn’t Matter Where,’ Children Say)”

Zoe: *sees headline on feed* “Ugh. Is this because that coach was in the news?”

Owen: “Yeah. Covered up for ten years. The athletic director knew all along.”

Emma: “This site always nails it. I can’t believe they even have a news-related reason to write this.”

Above, Zoe, Owen, and Emma implicitly agree that the justification for tolerating the humor attempt is greater than the justification for censuring it. When enough people feel this way, the satire itself won't be condemned.

Consider:

No one felt much humor in any of the examples above—but humor attempts can be fitted whether or not anyone actually feels humor.

Protection. When satire is tolerated because of fittedness, it's because of fittedness—not because it's satire. Similarly, if the English department censures an unfitted proverb, it's not because it's a proverb but because it's unfitted.

It's not genres that get a special blank check—it's just that those genres are often fitted and above the humor dress code. It's fittedness that determines whether any emotional attempt is justified and tolerated.
