The Humor Book

We can push toward tripwires fast or slowly

It's hard to guard tripwires against high-velocity movement:

Emma: (disbelief) “You can't do a handstand!”

Zoe: (amused) "I've been able to do them for years!" (←Zoe makes light push toward support an argument)

Emma: *scoffs* (unconvinced) "Uh-huh. I guess I can do them too!"

- OR -

Zoe: (amused) *does handstand* (←Zoe makes hard push toward support an argument)

Emma: (stunned) *laughs* "What!? What!?"

When Zoe actually does a handstand, she irresistably supports her argument (that she can do a handstand. The lack of room to question or dispute this means Emma can't prevent the situation from reaching the support an argument tripwire.

We increase velocity toward humor outcomes by minimizing interpretive ambiguity or slack—we rush a tripwire:

Minh matches with Amaya on a dating app.

Amaya: *texting* “Gosh you’re cute.” (←Amaya minimizes ambiguity or slack↔︎Amaya rushes toward create rapport, deal with taboo topics, open new topics of conversation, reveal a personal view, and so on↔︎high velocity)

Minh: *flushes* *smiles* (←Minh feels Amaya created rapport, dealt with taboo topics, opened new topics of conversation, revealed a personal view, and so on)

- OR -

Minh matches with Amaya on a dating app.

Amaya: *texting* “When I saw your picture, I immediately thought of something that rhymes with chute.” (←Amaya allows ambiguity or slack↔︎Amaya doesn't rush toward create rapport, deal with taboo topics, open new topics of conversation, reveal a personal view, and so on↔︎low velocity)

Minh: (confused) *smiles* (←Minh feels some humor at Amaya’s pun)

We can be rushed across a tripwire so suddenly that we find ourselves compelled to laugh:

Tutor: *finishes reading Zoe’s grad school application essay* *sighs* “This is pretty terrible.” (←tutor minimizes ambiguity or slack↔︎rushes toward humor outcomes↔︎high velocity)

Zoe: (shocked) *laughs* (←Zoe feels tutor revealed a personal view) “Really? Oh no.”

- OR -

Tutor: *finishes reading Zoe’s grad school application essay* *sighs* “Hm. Well. There are some things I think are really good, and other things that, well, you know. But really, most good, I mean that. We just gotta drill down a bit on genre and goals.” (←tutor allows ambiguity or slack↔︎doesn't rush toward humor outcomes↔︎low velocity)

Zoe: (confused) “So I’m on the right track?” (←Zoe is unsure of what the tutor is trying to say)

Yasmin’s mother: *without asking* *sets down a glass of water* “Here, I got you some water!” (←Yasmin's mother minimizes ambiguity or slack↔︎rushes toward humor outcomes↔︎high velocity)

Trevor: “Oh. Thank you.” *smiles* (←Trevor feels accepted)

- OR -

Yasmin’s mother: “Can we get you anything? Some tea, pop, water?” (←Yasmin's mother allows ambiguity or slack↔︎doesn't rush toward humor outcomes↔︎low velocity)

Trevor: *shakes head* “No thank you, I’m good.” (←Trevor doesn’t feel accepted)

Yasmin’s mother: “No really, you’d make me feel happy to get you something. What would you like?” (←pushes a bit harder toward help Trevor become accepted)

Trevor: *smiles* “Well, I guess I wouldn’t mind some soda.” (←Trevor feels accepted)

Trevor: *out of the blue* “What do you think about having a kid?” (←Trevor minimizes ambiguity or slack↔︎rushes toward humor outcomes↔︎high velocity)

Yasmin: *puts hand on face* *laughs* (←Yasmin feels Trevor has opened a new topic of conversation) “Oh god, Trevor. That’s not StormConQuest!”

- OR -

Trevor: *out of the blue* “Look, I’m going to bring up something and I don’t want you to freak out or anything.” (←Trevor allows ambiguity or slack↔︎doesn't rush toward humor outcomes↔︎low velocity)

Yasmin: *listens impassively* (←Trevor’s prelude shrinks her OK and grows her mistake)

Trevor: (nervous) *pressing on* “I know you haven’t wanted me to bring it up before. I mean, it’s scary and weird, and we both have plans and, I dunno, maybe I’m an asshole for bringing it up, and, I guess, well…” *clears throat* “I mean, have you ever… thought about kids?”

Yasmin: *sighs* “I don’t really want to talk about this now, Trevor” (←mistake is not OK↔︎Yasmin does not feel Trevor has opened a new topic of conversation)
