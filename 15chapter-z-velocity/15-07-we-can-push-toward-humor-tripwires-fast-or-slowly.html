The Humor Book

15. Velocity

We can expand our spatial metaphor of humor by thinking about the situational ball as having velocity.

The harder we press toward humor outcomes, the more velocity on the ball. The lighter we press, the less velocity:

Jennifer is getting ready to go to the store.

Sam: *from armchair* *not getting up* "I can go with you if you like!" (←Sam makes light push toward symbolize close ties)

- OR -

Sam: *gets up* *puts on jacket* "You know what, I'd love to come along." (←Sam makes hard push toward symbolize close ties)

Amber welcomes a potential new client.

Amber: "Hi! Glad you could make it. Stephen will be right right you." *Amber goes back to work* (←Amber makes light push toward help others become accepted)

- OR -

Amber: "Hi! Glad you could make it. Stephen will be right right you."

Amber pauses her work to briefly chat with the new client. She smiles and laughs, and offers him coffee. (←Amber makes hard push toward help others become accepted)

Emma has been hunched over her laptop for seven straight hours, working on her final project.

Zoe: *coming in* (concerned) "We should do some stretching or something when you're done!" (←Zoe makes light push toward easing tension)

- OR -

Zoe: *coming in* (concerned) *massages Emma's back* (←Zoe makes hard push toward easing tension)

Middle School Emma: "Ryan? Sometimes I think he's a... you know." (←Emma makes light push toward revealing a personal view)

- OR -

Middle School Emma: "Ryan? He's a moron!" (←Emma makes hard push toward revealing a personal view)

Above, when someone was direct about reaching a humor outcome, they increased the velocity. When they were indirect, they decreased the velocity.

We'll be talking about this throughout this chapter.

Actions. We often think of humor as involving speech, but it more often than not involves actions. We can symbolize close ties with our friend or partner through actions more concretely than by just words alone.

When Zoe takes the initiative to massage Emma or Sam happily puts on his coat to run an errand with Jennifer, these actions directly embody or lead to humor outcomes. The speech was neither here nor there.

In terms of our spatial metaphor, it's actions or consequential words that put velocity on the situational ball toward emotional outcomes—which is why Zoe and Sam just saying things can be treated as more inconsequential.
