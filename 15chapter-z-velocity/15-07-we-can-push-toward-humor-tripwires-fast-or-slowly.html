15. Velocity

Last chapter, we looked at humor outcome tripwires. In this chapter, we'll expand this spatial metaphor by looking at different degrees of pressure toward these tripwires.

The situational ball has velocity—hard or light movement toward humor outcome tripwires:

Sometimes we're pressed hard toward humor outcomes, and sometimes we're pressed light:

Yasmin’s mother: *without asking* *sets down a glass of water* “Here, I got you some water!” (←pushes hard toward making Trevor become accepted)

Trevor: “Oh. Thank you.” *smiles* (←Trevor feels accepted)

- OR -

Yasmin’s mother: “Can we get you anything? Some tea, pop, water?” (←pushes lightly toward help Trevor become accepted)

Trevor: *shakes head* “No thank you, I’m good.” (←Trevor doesn’t feel accepted)

Yasmin’s mother: “No really, you’d make me feel happy to get you something. What would you like?” (←pushes a bit harder toward help Trevor become accepted)

Trevor: *smiles* “Well, I guess I wouldn’t mind some soda.” (←Trevor feels accepted)

We'll be talking about this throughout this chapter.
