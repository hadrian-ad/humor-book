The Humor Book

We use the velocity we think will be effective

If we’re hanging out with our friend, we may not need much velocity to reach humor outcome tripwires.

Consider:

Our friend would be more indulgent with letting us control a situation through spontaneous whims or free association than our manager would.

But if we’re trying to derail a serious conversation by making one of us laugh, we may need something that makes a more focused push toward humor outcomes:

Heather: *hands on hips* “Xiaowei, when are you going to start looking for a job?”

Xiaowei: *playing a game* (listlessly) “When my shit turns blue.” (←Xiaowei makes a hard push toward humor outcomes↔︎high velocity)

Heather: *throws hands up* (←Heather feels Xiaowei is being flippant)

Xiaowei: *laughs* (←mistakes and OKs match↔︎humor)

- OR -

Heather: *hands on hips* “Xiaowei, when are you going to start looking for a job?”

Xiaowei: *playing a game* *trying to make a joke on the fly* “Mom, not until the minimum wage is the drinking age—the chant of my generation. Give us booze or boosted wages, haha.” (←Xiaowei makes a meandering, light push toward humor outcomes↔︎low velocity)

Heather: “Well, what about Justin’s brother? He runs a carpet store, you’d earn more than minimum wage.” (←joke doesn’t have enough velocity to derail conversation)

Xiaowei: *sighs* *slowly* “Justin’s brother’s carpet shop…” (←Xiaowei’s comment didn’t push the situation across any humor tripwires↔︎no humor)

When Xiaowei rushes the tripwires, the high-velocity catches Heather off-guard, letting Xiaowei reach a humor outcome and feel humor.

By contrast, when Xiawei focuses on constructing a conventional joke than he did avoiding conflict or controlling the situation, he didn't achieve enough velocity to affect the situation.

Consider:

This doesn’t mean a lighter push of some kind by Xiaowei couldn’t have worked—or that the harder push worked in a strategic sense. Instead, we're looking at why Xiaowei's intuition might have led him to make a sharper comment.
