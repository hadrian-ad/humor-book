We should notice all intensities of humor

Humor tells convey intensity, like how a smile can be low-intensity (light smile) or high-intensity (beaming):

Sometimes we only focus on the more-intense half of the spectrum—like broad smiles that crest into laughter. But we should also focus on the less-intense half of the spectrum:

When we see humor everywhere it occurs, we can better understand how people relate to what's happening around them—as well better acclimate to all the various humor profiles around us.

We wouldn't be very good accountants if we didn't realize that cents were money—or if we thought we never had to account for them. Something similar happens with humor.

If we didn't realize a twinge in our cheeks was a smile—or thought we never had to bother paying attention to these smiles—we would have a blind spot in our observations. When we can instead track and notice these low-intensity variations of a smile, we can better pick up nuance and changes in what's happening around us.
