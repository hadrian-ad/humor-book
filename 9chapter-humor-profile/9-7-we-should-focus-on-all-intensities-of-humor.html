We should notice all intensities of humor

Humor tells convey intensity, like how a smile can be low-intensity (light smile) or high-intensity (beaming):

Sometimes we only focus on the more-intense half of the spectrum—like broad smiles that crest into laughter. But we should also focus on the less-intense half of the spectrum:

When we see humor everywhere it occurs, we can better understand how people relate to what's happening around them—as well as allowing us to better acclimate to all the various humor profiles around us.

We wouldn't be very good accountants if we didn't realize that cents were money—or if we thought we never had to account for them. Something similar happens with humor.

If we didn't realize a twinge in our cheeks was a smile—or thought we never had to bother noticing them—we would have a blind spot in our observations. When we're able to follow these low-intensity variations, we can pick up nuance about what's happening around us.
