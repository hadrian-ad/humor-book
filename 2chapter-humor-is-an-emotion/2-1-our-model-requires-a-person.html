The Humor Book

Our model requires a person

Since humor is an emotion, a specific person feels humor. Different people may feel different things—one person may feel humor, while another may feel something else:

Xiaowei is dropping his friend Lina off at their high school for the opening day of her play.

Xiaowei: "Break a leg! Also: You look nervous as hell!"

Lina gives him the finger on the way out. (←Lina doesn't feel humor) Xiaowei laughs and gives the finger back. (←Xiaowei feels humor)

Elias stops by to give Jessica and Emma a ride, but Jessica is running a bit behind. When she finally comes out of the bathroom, Elias makes a belittling comment about the slogan on Jessica’s shirt. (←Elias feels humor)

It makes Jessica laugh. (←Jessica feels humor) It makes Emma furious. (←Emma doesn't feel humor)

Bleary-eyed from staying up late, Zoe starts giggling. (←Zoe feels humor) Emma puts her head in her hands.

Emma: (despairing) “We’ll never finish,” (←Emma doesn't feel humor)

Amber visits Jennifer, and laughs at the cat wedged in a cardboard box. (←Amber feels humor) But Jennifer has seen the cat there so often, she no longer notices anything. (←Jennifer doesn't feel humor)

Since these outcomes are different for each person, we’ll need to be thoughtful about which perspective we’re looking at:

  • Humor is an emotion Xiaowei feels when he evaluates a mistake as OK.
  • Humor is an emotion Yasmin feels when she evaluates a mistake as OK.
  • Humor is an emotion Jessica feels when she evaluates a mistake as OK.
  • Humor is an emotion Zoe feels when she evaluates a mistake as OK.
  • Humor is an emotion Amber feels when she evaluates a mistake as OK.

Because of this, we can update our model:

Humor is an emotion that someone feels when they evaluate a mistake as OK.
