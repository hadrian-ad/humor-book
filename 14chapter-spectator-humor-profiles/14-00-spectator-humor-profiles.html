Chapter 14

Spectator Humor Profile

In this chapter, we’ll look at spectator humor profiles—how artists, actors, characters, and so on portray humor in spectator entertainment.

Consider:

If we're playing an evil villain in a play and have to laugh, we won't laugh like ourselves (personal humor profile)—but like a play actor (spectator humor profile).

We can usually distinquish between the evaluations of spectators, actors, and characters:

Sam and Jennifer are attending Riley's school play.

Riley and Maddie are playing the Eager Acorns. As they run on stage, but briefly get in each other's way. They grin at each other. (←personal humor profile)

Sam and Jennifer both smile. (←personal humor profile)

Acorns: *finally reaching center stage* "Old Oak, you are so OLD you don't understand us acorns, hahahahaha!" (←spectator humor profile)

Jennifer smiles as she writes Sam a little short story. (←personal humor profile)

In it, a character spills milk and laughs. (←spectator humor profile)

Sam reads it later and smiles. (←personal humor profile)

A security guard approaches Sam and Jennifer.

Sam smiles. (←personal humor profile)

Jennifer is stressed. She gives a social smile, which Sam notices immediately as forced. (←spectator humor profile)

Sam is watching The Trolby Report

Trolby: (in character) "Our enemies say our campaign for city council doesn't have support. Ha! Haha!" (←spectator humor profile) "Well, try saying that to these donors!"

Trolby starts reading off his donor list. It's just a list of fake names.

The comedian loses his composure and starts laughing. He turns red as he holds paper over his mouth to disguise his laughter. (←personal humor profile)

Sam smiles. (←personal humor profile)

Sam and Jennifer are watching a romantic movie.

The main characters are on sleds at the top of a hill. They smile at each other. (←Spectator humor profile)

They start going down, and the characters' eyes light up. When the woman gets sprayed with powder, she laughs. It's clear the actors are actually having fun. (←personal humor profile)

Sam and Jennifer smile. It's a cute opening. (←personal humor profile)

Sam is watching bloopers for Blood Soul Revenge.

Daisy Girl: "Hah!" (←spectator humor profile) *to usurper* (defiant) “You’ll never find Rage Man. He’s a bladed shadow. He’s crimson moonlight. He treads the mmhmm…” *forgets line*

She throws up her hands and laughs. (←personal humor profiles)

Sam smiles. (←personal humor profile)

We can distinquish between people experiencing humor and people pretending to experience humor.

We'll be talking more about this throughout this chapter.
