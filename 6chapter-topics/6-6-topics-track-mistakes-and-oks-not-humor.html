The Humor Book

Topics track mistakes and OKs, not humor

Topics track mistakes and OKs. These evaluations sometimes generate humor, but don't always. 

Some of our topics may never generate humor at all—like how the topic ‘houseplant’ might never produce any humor our entire lives: 

But once we estimate the size of the mistake and OK on a topic, we have the information we need to try to generate humor from them.

For instance, since ‘houseplant’ has a large OK, we could try using a large mistake to generate humor. We could: 

  • pretend it's a therapist and talk to it
  • focus on it to try to see it in new light
  • pretend our houseplant is a reincarnated white pine
  • give it a name like Augusta or Lord Grom
  • take many pictures of it wearing a scarf and share them with a friend

(and so on)

These would be larger mistakes that may match the large OK—and so generate humor.

When we watch comedic movies, we sometimes feel humor in a way that seems to take no effort of our own. Things are a bit different with something like a houseplant—involving a houseplant in humor often takes effort and initiative.

As we get better at generating humor on specific topics, we become more versatile at humor—and become more precise at emotional outcomes for different topics.

For instance:

Once we can generate humor from a houseplant, we can generate humor anywhere, on any topic. We're not limited to just "funny" topics in comedies—since any topic can have balanced mistakes and OKs on it.

It takes some practice and effective methodology to balance mistakes and OKs like this. But we can practice, because it's a skill—it's something we can improve at!
