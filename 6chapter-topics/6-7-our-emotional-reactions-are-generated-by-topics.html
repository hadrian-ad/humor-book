Topics make our emotional reactions

We'll use topics in this book to discuss emotions—whether emotionally straightforward topics (‘houseplant’) or emotionally complex clusters of topics (‘Jennifer’s illness’).

For instance, when Yasmin gets a job offer, she feels all kinds of things:

Yasmin gets a job offer in Columbus.

A. The idea of moving to a big city for a better job is exciting, and it’s something that makes her laugh. (← mistake is OK↔︎humor)

B. On the other hand, it’s also terrifying! It makes her feel incredibly sober and nervous. (←mistake is not OK↔︎no humor)

C. But then she thinks about how cool it is she actually got the job offer?! She laughs and feels powerful for a while. (←mistake is OK↔︎humor)

D. Then she wrings her hands. What about her family here? What about Trevor? Why would she leave them? (←mistake is not OK↔︎no humor)

E. But won’t she have money now to go visit? She imagines having discretionary income, and laughs. (←mistake is OK↔︎humor)

She goes back and forth all day.
