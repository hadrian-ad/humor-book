Topics determine fittedness

Fittedness increases when humor or humor attempts have relevant topics, and decreases when there's irrelevant topics:

Artem is waiting for Alina at her lab. Hana asks if he’s related to Alina, and, shyly, he says yes.

Hana: (appreciatively) “Alina is really good at what she does.”

Artem: (awkwardly) “Yeah.”

There’s a moment of silence while Artem tries to think of something to say.

Artem: “You know why elevators like libraries so much?”

Hana: “Why?”

Artem: “Because libraries have so many stories.” (←joke on irrelevant topics makes humor attempt less fitted)

Hana: *smiles* “Haha. Good one.”

- OR -

Artem: “She is a great DJ!” (←Artem generates humor while continuing topic ‘Alina’)

Hana: *laughs*

- OR -

Artem: “You must be good at what you do too, since you’re here! That’s so cool that you guys do all this cool stuff here!” (←Artem includes other relevant topics like ‘Hana,’ ‘electrical engineering’, ‘lab,’ and so on, and tries to make Hana feel achievement)

Hana: *smiles* “Haha, it’s not that impressive, but thanks.” (←Hana feels achievement)

We can order these three jokes by order from least to most fitted:

The one that followed up on recently relevant topics was most fitted.

Consider:

When something is more fitted, that doesn't mean it's the best option, funniest, most important, or so on—it just means its most fitted.

Focus. It's the same process to find the fittedness of humor (an emotion) as it is to find the fittedness of a humor attempt (an action).

Even if Hana never smiles in any of the examples above (humor attempts without humor), we could still organize the humor attempts by fittedness.

Similarly, even if Hana smiles at Artem without Artem knowing she's there (humor without humor attempts), we could still organize her humor by fittedness—it's more fitted if she smiles because she recognizes him than because she thinks about cake.
