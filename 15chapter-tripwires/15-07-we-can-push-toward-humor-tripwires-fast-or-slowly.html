The Humor Book

2. Humor is an emotion

We can push toward humor outcomes with different degrees of pressure. Sometimes we make hard pushes to humor outcomes, and sometimes lighter pushes:

Yasmin’s mother: *without asking* *sets down a glass of water* “Here, I got you some water!” (←pushes hard toward help Trevor become accepted)

Trevor: “Oh. Thank you.” *smiles* (←Trevor feels accepted)

- OR -

Yasmin’s mother: “Want some tea, pop, water?” (←pushes lightly toward help Trevor become accepted)

Trevor: *shakes head* “No thank you, I’m good.” (←Trevor doesn’t feel accepted)

Yasmin’s mother: “No really, you’d make me feel happy to get you something. What would you like?” (←pushes a bit harder toward help Trevor become accepted)

Trevor: *smiles* “Well, I guess I wouldn’t mind some soda.” (←Trevor feels accepted)

This hard or light push is the “velocity” of the situational ball:

When we are direct and insistent on reaching a humor outcome, we increase the velocity. When we’re indirect and tentative, we decrease the velocity:

Minh matches with Amaya on a dating app.

Amaya: *texting* “Gosh you’re cute.” (←pushes hard toward reveal a personal view↔︎high velocity)

Minh: *flushes* *smiles* (←Minh feels Amaya revealed a personal view)

- OR -

Minh matches with Amaya on a dating app.

Amaya: *texting* “When I saw your picture, I immediately thought of something that rhymes with chute.” (←pushes lightly toward reveal a personal view↔︎low velocity)

Minh: (confused) *smiles* (←Minh feels some humor at Amaya’s pun)

When the situational ball has high velocity, it can be hard to defend a tripwire. This is when we find ourselves feeling compelled to laugh, or laugh almost without even realizing it:

Tutor: *finishes reading Zoe’s grad school application essay* *sighs* “This is pretty terrible.” (←pushes hard toward humor outcomes quickly↔︎high velocity)

Zoe: (shocked) *laughs* (←Zoe feels tutor acceptably criticized her) “Really? Oh no.”

- OR -

Tutor: *finishes reading Zoe’s grad school application essay* *sighs* “Hm. Well. There are some things I think are really good, and other things that, well, you know. But really, most good, I mean that. We just gotta drill down a bit on genre and goals.” (←pushes lightly toward humor outcomes ←low velocity)

Zoe: (confused) “So I’m on the right track?” (←Zoe is unsure of what the tutor is trying to say)

Trevor: *out of the blue* “What do you think about having a kid?” (←Trevor rushes toward opening a new topic of conversation↔︎high velocity)

Yasmin: *puts hand on face* *laughs* (←Yasmin feels Trevor has opened a new topic of conversation) “Oh god, Trevor. That’s not StormConQuest!”

- OR -

Trevor: *out of the blue* “Look, I’m going to bring up something and I don’t want you to freak out or anything.” (←Trevor meandors toward humor outcomes↔︎low velocity)

Yasmin: *listens impassively* (←Trevor’s prelude shrinks her OK and grows her mistake

Trevor: (nervous) *pressing on* “I know you haven’t wanted me to bring it up before. I mean, it’s scary and weird, and we both have plans and, I dunno, maybe I’m an asshole for bringing it up, and, I guess, well…” *clears throat* “I mean, have you ever… thought about kids?”

Yasmin: *sighs* “I don’t really want to talk about this now, Trevor” (←mistake is not OK↔︎Yasmin does not feel Trevor has opened a new topic of conversation)
