The Humor Book

Everyone has influence over tripwires

Everyone plays a part in whether or not humor outcomes are reached:

Heather: *hands on hips* “Xiaowei, when are you going to start looking for a job?”

Xiaowei: *playing a game* (listlessly) “When my shit turns blue.” (←Xiaowei pushes the situation toward controlling the situation, handling a complaint, and so on)

Heather: *sits down* *crosses legs* (calmly) “How are you feeling lately?” (←deflects the situation away from those tripwires)

Xiaowei: (annoyed) “Annoyed, right now.” (←Heather preempts Xiaowei from feeling he controlled the situation, handled a complaint, or avoided conflict↔︎no humor)

Heather prevents Xiaowei from feeling humor—by preventing him from feeling like he's avoided conflict, controlled the situation, handled complaints, and so on.

Consider:

This isn’t to say that this is the “best” thing for Heather to do. We’re just looking at how the humor in a situation isn’t predetermined by one person—since both people are involved in creating or preventing humor.

Xiaowei can't unilaterally handle (Heather's) complaint because he's in a multilateral situation—he and Heather together triangulate whether he feels he handles (Heather's) complaint or not.
