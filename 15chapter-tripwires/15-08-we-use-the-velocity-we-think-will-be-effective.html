The Humor Book

We use the velocity we think will be effective

We’ll use the velocity that we think will be effective. If we’re hanging out with our friend, we may not need much velocity to reach humor outcome tripwires.

For instance:

Our friends are more indulgent with letting us control a situation ("should we go get ice cream?") than a stranger or our manager would be.

By contrast, if we’re trying to derail a conversation, we’ll need something that makes a harder push toward humor outcomes:

Heather: *hands on hips* “Xiaowei, when are you going to start looking for a job?”

Xiaowei: *playing a game* (listlessly) “When my shit turns blue.” (←Xiaowei makes a hard push toward humor outcomes↔︎high velocity)

Heather: *throws hands up* (←Heather feels Xiaowei is being flippant)

Xiaowei: *laughs* (←mistakes and OKs match↔︎humor)

- OR -

Heather: *hands on hips* “Xiaowei, when are you going to start looking for a job?”

Xiaowei: *playing a game* *trying to make a joke on the fly* “Mom, not until the minimum wage is the drinking age—the chant of my generation. Give us booze or boosted wages, haha.” (←Xiaowei makes a meandering, light push toward humor outcomes↔︎low velocity)

Heather: “Well, what about Justin’s brother? He runs a carpet store, you’d earn more than minimum wage.” (←joke doesn’t have enough velocity to derail conversation)

Xiaowei: *sighs* (slowly) “Justin’s brother’s carpet shop…” (←Xiaowei’s comment didn’t push the situation across any humor tripwires↔︎no humor)

Xiaowei “rushes” the tripwires by not thinking about humor—instead, he only focuses on avoiding conflict, controlling a situation, handling a complaint, and so on. This high velocity catches Heather off-guard, and allows him to feel humor.

Consider:

By contrast, the more conventional joke didn’t have enough velocity to affect the situation—which is why Xiaowei avoided the low-velocity option.

This doesn’t mean a lighter push couldn’t have worked—or that the harder push worked in a strategic sense. Instead, we're looking at why Xiaowei's intuition led him to make a sharper comment.
