Chapter 10

Spectator Entertainment

In this chapter, we’ll look at spectator entertainment. These are things that prioritize the emotions of spectators.

Emma and Owen watch action movie. (←movie is spectator entertainment)

Artem reads a manga. (←manga is spectator entertainment)

Jennifer examines an advertisement for dish soap. (←advertisement is spectator entertainment)

Sam and Jennifer rehearse for a play. (←play is spectator entertainment)

When Sam and Jennifer perform their play, it doesn't matter so much how they feel or how their characters feel—it matters how their spectators feel.

Since the priority is on the experience of the spectators, the play is spectator entertainment.

We'll be talking about this throughout this chapter.

Conversion. We can orient something between ourselves (interspersonal orientation) or orient it for others (spectator orientation). This is like the difference between competitive wrestling and dramatized wrestling:

  • Competitive wrestling is intense and interesting to participants—but not always as interesting to spectators. Casual spectators might see the wrestlers in odd-looking helmets and uniforms locked in a static grapple for a long time before something happens quickly and the match is over.
  • Dramatized wrestling is designed to be intense and interesting to the audience—and how the participants feel is less important. In order to better commercialize wrestling, showrunners staged and performed scenes in a way that was easy to follow, exciting, visually stimulating, and so on—for the audience.
