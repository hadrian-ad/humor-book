Defining humor

In this chapter, we'll begin defining humor:

Zoe, Emma, and Owen are walking through campus when they see a sign that says DANGER, with the warning whited out.

Chalk: "Unreadable signs cause..."

Zoe & Emma: *laugh* (←humor)

Owen taps his foot. (←no humor)

Jennifer and Sam take their daughter Riley to a zoo. They see a sign that says GOATS HAVE NO MANNERS.

Jennifer & Sam: *laugh* (←humor)

Riley: "What? What?" (←no humor)

Dave taxis through another country and laughs at the stop sign. (← humor)

The taxi driver doesn't laugh. (←no humor)

Some people might smile and laugh at the signs below. But why only some people, some of the time?

Sometimes we think there's no rhyme or reason to laughter. But there's a universal pattern to when we smile and laugh.

In this chapter, we'll introduce this pattern at its simplest, and we'll increasingly layer in more complexity to this pattern throughout this book.

Sequence. This book is designed to be read in order, so that we can layer in understanding based on what's come before. It's likely that at some point we'll read something and think "well, what about...?"

We'll probably get to it soon! Just keep reading, and we'll get to implied considerations.
