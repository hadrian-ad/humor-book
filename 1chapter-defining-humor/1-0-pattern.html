Chapter 1

Humor Pattern

When do we smile and laugh? When we stop to think about it, we'll notice that we smile and laugh in a bewildering variety of contexts— including when we:

  • are given a compliment
  • are insulted
  • are hugged
  • hear a joke
  • receive a gift
  • are kissed
  • bike downhill
  • come to a scenic overlook
  • put clothes on fresh out of the drier
  • realize we've been foolish
  • are pressured successfully
  • feel a gust of wind
  • finish a workout
  • see a fox
  • win a game
  • lose badly
  • enter a beautiful room
  • see something disgusting
  • recall an embarrassing memory

and so on

To make matters more confusing, we sometimes don't smile or laugh at these things. Other times, we even laugh at their opposites!

For instance:

We may laugh if we suceed or fail. On the other hand, we could also get upset when we succeed or fail instead.

What's the pattern to all this, that explains the presence or absence of humor in all cases?

In this chapter, we'll be looking at the pattern to when and why we smile and laugh—which we can use to think through any instance of humor.

We'll introduce the basics of this pattern this chapter.

Beginnings. Even though geometry starts at the beginning, it's not remedial instruction in "shapes"—it builds a practical foundation.

When students first take geometry, it can be strange to them to study something as simple and universal as a rectangle or triangle. And from one perspective, it's true—rectangles and triangles are simple, profoundly intuitive shapes.

At the same time, there's more to learn about rectangles and triangles than students often suspect at the outset—and the study of angles and area opens up all kinds of practical skills.

We can think about smiling and laughing as similar. Even though this book starts "at the beginning," it's not remedial instruction in humor. Instead, it's the beginning of something deeper than we might suspect at the outset—the "angles and area" to humor dynamics between people.

This book assumes you have a lifetime of observations and experiences with humor, and then provides catalytic insight to organize it all in a coherent, workable system.
