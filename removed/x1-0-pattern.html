Chapter 1

Humor Pattern

In this book, humor refers to laughing and smiling—it's something that happens, or is felt. Our first goal is learn when and why we smile and laugh.

Before we go over the pattern to humor, it's worth noticing the endless variety of situations that sometimes have laughter. We laugh when we:

  • are given a compliment
  • are insulted
  • are hugged
  • hear a joke
  • receive a gift
  • are kissed
  • bike downhill
  • come to a scenic overlook
  • put clothes on fresh out of the drier
  • realize we've been foolish
  • are pressured successfully
  • feel a gust of wind
  • finish a workout
  • see a fox
  • win a game
  • lose badly
  • enter a beautiful room
  • see something disgusting
  • recall an embarrassing memory

and so on

To make matters more confusing, we sometimes don't laugh at these things—other times, we even laugh at their opposites.

What's the pattern to all this, that explains the presence or absence of humor in all cases?

Sometimes we think there is no pattern to humor. But, in fact, we can find an elegant pattern—which we'll be introducing this chapter.
