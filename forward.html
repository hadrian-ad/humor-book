Forward

Before I read Humor in Various Moods and Meters for the first time, I'll have to admit I was skeptical. The subtitle was Instruction on Observing Smiles and Laughter. Did I really need this instruction? Do you?

After reading this book several times—I can say that yes, we all need this instruction. Perhaps now more than ever.

I've guided several students of mine to this book, and each has attested to the same pleasing quality of "surprising obviousness" to the observations in this book.

What this book does is invite readers to look again at patterns of smiling and laughing (what the author calls "humor"). After a well-paced guided tour, readers leave with the sticky sense that the world is more beautiful and elegant than we had grasped.

I hope you find this sticky sense of beauty and elegance too.

Frederick Toolsson
