The Humor Book

The Humor Book is a clean course on humor—a learning resource on smiling and laughing that's accessible, clear, coherent, practical, and straightforward.

By starting at the beginning—and simply reading progressively to the end—you'll learn an elegant and productive model of humor.

About

Hey! I'm Hadrian, the author of this book. I'm a humor theorist with a specific interest in teaching humor. To everyone.

Sometimes people are unsure if they need to learn about humor. They often ask me something like: "I'm a pretty normal person! I smile, I 'get' jokes. Do I really need to learn more about humor?"

And my reply is always: Everyone should learn more about humor! There's a lifetime dynamic going on, centered on something with no skill ceiling. That's why the best and worst people at humor are both still learning about it—just like we are.

Even though this book starts "at the beginning," it's not remedial instruction. Instead, this book provides catalytic insight to our experiences with humor, helping us organize a lifetime of observations into a coherent system.

This is a course on humor

Some humor books are fun because they expertly tell anecdotes in an amusing style. This humor book is fun because it's a textbook. You know what I mean?

This book is a course—it teaches something: an analytical model of humor.

We'll expand this model into a methodological and observational system to make sense of what's happening around us, and why.

We won't just talk about these things, but actually learn them too—by using thousands of vignettes, hundreds of graphs, dozens of case studies, and plenty of picture and video examples.

This is a brisk course of moderate length. It takes most readers in the ballpark of 20 hours to complete. Most readers find the time spent to be enjoyable, interesting, empowering, and personally relevant.

Unlike many textbooks, which are designed to be modular—skipped around as needed—this book is meant to be read from beginning to end, just like a normal book.

The elephant in the room

One of the challenges to learning about humor is just how big it is. It's a bit like the story of the Blind Mice and the Elephant. The blind mice come across a mysterious object, and each touch it to see what it is:

  • One blind mouse runs up the leg and says “it’s a tree trunk!”
  • Another goes up the tail, and says “it’s a rope!”
  • The last clambers around the ear and says “it’s a curtain!”

This is like humor! At different times, to different people, humor is seen as:

  • sometimes comedy, sometimes tragedy
  • sometimes frivolous, sometimes important
  • sometimes mean, sometimes kind
  • sometimes related to anxiety, sometimes to confidence
  • sometimes related to shame, sometimes to pride
  • sometimes related to failure, sometimes to success
  • sometimes related to jokes, sometimes to straight-talk
  • sometimes about lies, sometimes about truth
  • sometimes about randomness, and sometimes about insight

(and so on)

What sort of elephant is this? It's like having a jigsaw puzzle with no box art! This book will put all these pieces harmoniously together, so that we can step back at the end and see what this humor thing really looks like.

Like any good puzzle, there are solutions that, while not initially obvious, are elegant. We'll arrive at this elegance by unfolding an explanation of humor piece-by-piece, chapter-by-chapter.

Each chapter focuses one thing at a time—which sometime means it can seem to leave something out. We'll get to those things in later chapters!

Goals

I see humor as substantially and practically composing our "aura of ability"— in other words, our aggragate ability to:

  • ask for and give aid
  • adapt and be resilient
  • convince others or effectively share viewpoints
  • create things in ways that people (including ourselves) appreciate
  • enjoy new and old things
  • form acquaintances and grow closer to them
  • interpret situations
  • participate effectively in discussions and organizations

(and so on)

When we learn about humor, we learn about the process and methods in which all these things happen!

Our goal is to understand how this all works—and how to do it all better, relative to wherever we are now.

In this book, "humor" refers to the process through which smiles or laughs do or don't happen. We all have different levels of skill in different situations with this process—but the more familiar we are with this process, the better our actions and decisions tend to be.
