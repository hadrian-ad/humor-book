The Humor Textbook

A course on laughter, people, and joy

The Humor Book is a clean course on humor—a learning resource on smiling and laughing that's accessible, clear, coherent, practical, and straightforward.

By starting at the beginning—and simply reading progressively to the end—you'll learn an elegant and productive model of humor.

About

Hey! I'm Hadrian, the author of this book. I'm a humor theorist with a specific interest in teaching humor. To everyone.

Everyone should learn about humor!

Sometimes people are unsure if they need to learn about humor. They often say something like: "I'm a pretty normal person. I smile, I 'get' jokes. So do I really need to learn more about humor?"

And my reply is always: everyone should learn more about humor! Humor is something with no skill ceiling and few borders. There's a lifetime learning dynamic going on.

That's why the best and worst people at humor are both still learning about it—just like we are.

Even though this book starts "at the beginning," it's not remedial instruction. Instead, this book provides catalytic insight to our experiences with humor, helping us organize a lifetime of observations into a coherent system.

Who is this course for?

From experience, I can say this course has practical value to all kinds of people, including helping:

  • architects and interior designers think about why some arrangements lead to more smiles over time than others
  • facilitators and service workers better handle conflict and create compromise
  • musicians and painters make conscious creative choices leading to smiles
  • parents and children better understand each other
  • performers and public speakers find new perspectives on their audience
  • researchers and historians use a coherent analytical model of humor
  • romantic partners and family members more broadly generate laughter in people they care about
  • teachers and instructors gain insights about their class and methods
  • transplants and transfers better form connections with strangers, neighbors, and coworkers

(and so on)

Of course, while this course helps teachers gain insight or facilitators handle conflict, this course helps everyone who reads this textbook gain insight on people or handle conflict.

If improving at any of the skills and outcomes above interest you, this course is for you.

In this book, "humor" refers to the process through which smiles or laughs do or don't happen. That means this isn't a book about "jokes," but about something much broader.

For instance, we sometimes smile or laugh at success, failure, hugs, violence, cute things, gross things, jokes, compliments, and vegetables. This book will explain the presence or absence of smiling and laughing in all cases—which is inclusive to jokes, but also treats them as incidental.

This is a course on humor

This book isn't a series of anecdotes told in an amusing style—it's a course. It teaches something: an analytical model of humor.

We'll expand this model into a methodological and observational system. This will help us make sense of what's happening around us.

We won't just talk about these things, but actually learn them too—by using thousands of vignettes, hundreds of graphs, dozens and dozens of case studies, and plenty of picture and video examples.

This is a brisk course of moderate length. It takes most readers in the ballpark of 10 hours to complete. Most readers find the time spent to be enjoyable, interesting, empowering, and personally relevant.

Unlike many textbooks, which are designed to be modular—skipped around as needed—this book is meant to be read from beginning to end, just like a normal book. If we skip around, things won't make much sense.

You'll gain a lot from this course

I've come to see humor as substantially and practically composing our aura of ability. This is our aggragate ability to:

  • ask for and give aid
  • adapt and be resilient
  • convince others or effectively share viewpoints
  • create things in ways that people (including ourselves) appreciate
  • enjoy new and old things
  • form acquaintances and grow closer to them
  • interpret situations
  • participate effectively in discussions and organizations

(and so on)

Humor is tightly embedded into what makes these things happen. That means that, when we learn about humor, we learn about the process and methods in which all these things happen!

Our goal is to understand how this all works—and how to do it all better than we do right now.

There are many myths about humor—like that humor is abstract, unknowable, undefinable, unteachable, and so on. None of those are true. Usually, those statements describe the speaker more than they describe humor: "humor is unteachable (by me)."

Humor is a coherent process—so we can model it! We can use this model to describe and predict outcomes. This has transformational ramifications on how we see and use humor—leading to an improvement in our intuitive relation to humor.

It's almost time to start

Look, I'm so excited you're about to start! It's my hope that re-encountering humor—especially in this book's style and manner—provides you with greater observational strength, social agency, wonder, and joy.
