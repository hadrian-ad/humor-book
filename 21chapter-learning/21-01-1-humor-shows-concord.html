Humor shows concord in learning

We talked before about We talked before about how each emotional zone had a different timbre of influence. There's coercive influence (mistake-predominant influence) and intertial influence (OK-predominant influence). In-between them is concordant influence (humor influence).

Etymology:

Concord etymologically means something like "hearts (moving in the same direction) together."

Concord is what makes humor outcomes humor outcomes. We can be forced to gain perspectives by having someone set our house on fire—but we only concordantly gain perspectives when we feel humor.

This helps us understand what we mean when we say we only learn when we feel humor and vice versa. What's happening is that we feel an argument has been (concordantly) supported, new perspectives have been (concordantly) gained, other ideas have been (concordantly) criticized, complaints about other ideas have been (concordantly) handled, rapport has been (concordantly) created with us, and so on.

We may touch a hot stove and "learn" something, but this is a coerced gained perspective—it's not until a minute passes and we smile through our pain that the perspective starts to be concordantly gained.

Similarly, we can pass a driver's license examination by the boring memorization of pointless facts (“Begin braking at least 300 feet from a stop sign…”) This might either be coercive or intertial "learning," but in this book there's only one concordant way to learn—humor.

It's this concord that produces feeling of acceptance, achievement, comfort, insight, realization, revelation, safety, and so on, which constitute the mechanism of new perspectives being incorporated.
