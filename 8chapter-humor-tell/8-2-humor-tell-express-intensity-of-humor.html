Humor tells express intensity

Humor tells have low-intensity and high-intensity variations. This corresponds to the intensity of humor.

This is familiar with smiling. Sometimes we just half-smile, and other times we beam. Both beaming and half-smiling are the same humor tell (smiling) at different intensities:

The woman is giving a broad smile, the man a half-smile. The intensity of the humor tell corresponds to the intensity of their humor.

Sometimes, we only think of humor as high-intensity humor, like belly laughs. But humor can also be made up of low-intensity humor tells too, like in the pair below:

Laughing isn't humor. When enough humor tells happen together, it's humor. And while smiling or laughing is often one of those humor tells, they're not always part of humor. We can feel humor, even without smiling or laughing!

We may not smile or laugh as we:

  • walk along a low wall (non-normative behavior)
  • bob our head slightly to a song as we walk through a store (increased energy)
  • share a glance with a friend after the person we're talking to says something odd (social seeking)

(and so on)

These are all familiar instances where many of us feel humor without smiling or laughing—something that happens all the time, all across our lives!
