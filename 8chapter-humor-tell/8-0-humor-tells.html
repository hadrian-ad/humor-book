Humor tells

In previous chapters, we’ve said humor is an emotion. In this chapter, we’ll be looking more specifically at what this means. What does it mean to feel or experience humor?

To start, we’ll look at “humor tells.” When enough humor tells occur together, they compose humor.

Humor tells brightening energy expansive posture eyes twinkling flushing higher speaking register informal posture informal movement laughter loosening reduction of physical tension relaxed posture smiling touching others touching self efforts to spread humor fast thinking fast speaking louder voice focusing on present high energy increased agency loss of situational awareness non-normative behavior optimism raised spirits reduction of stress social integration social seeking social tightening (and so on) “Humor” and “humor tells” are the same thing: when we experience humor, these humor tells occur in clusters—and when humor tells occur in clusters, we experience humor. Above, the man brightens, smiles, has energy, informal posture, optimism, raised spirits, relaxed posture, lost situational awareness, is touching self, focusing on present, social seeking (looking at someone), and so on. These are all humor tells. Since there are so many humor tells occurring at once, we know the man is experiencing humor. Variations. Each humor tell refers to a wide range of behavior. For instance, touching self might refer to someone: leaning forward and putting their elbows on their thighs leaning back and clasping their hands above their belly putting a hand on their chin putting a hand on their forehead covering their face crossing their arms or legs clapping their hands slapping their thigh playing with their hair (and so on) Often, humor tells are shaped by circumstances. For instance, social seeking might mean sharing a glance—but it may also mean excitedly pulling out our phone to text someone (social seeking) or running into the other room to find someone (also social seeking). Since humor tells are so varied, our general list isn’t exhaustive or official—it’s just an observational starting point.
