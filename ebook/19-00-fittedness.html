Chapter 19

Fittedness

In this chapter, we’ll be looking at when and why humor is “appropriate.” When and why does humor gets pushback?

Owen’s chemistry report is returned to him. The professor had circled the title “O carbon, my carbon!” in red pen and took off five points. (←Owen’s humor attempt is seen as inappropriate)

Hana shares a meme that makes what she sees as humorous absurdist points about another country’s aggression. Her thesis advisor tells her to be careful. (←Hana’s association with humor attempt is seen as inappropriate)

Jessica: *coming in* “Did you hear Carl’s mom died?!”

Emma: “What, Mary? Oh my god.” *laughs*

Jessica: “Emma! You can’t laugh at that!” (←Jessica sees Emma’s humor as inappropriate)

Emma: (distressed) “I’m not laughing! That’s just terrible.”

Sabrina is arguing with herself about whether levity in a cover letter is appropriate. It seems impossible to her that an employer wouldn’t value a sense of humor in an employee. Eventually, she bows to conventional wisdom and deletes the funny phrase. (←Sabrina struggles to figure out the appropriateness of humor attempts)

Riley can’t stop making poop jokes and laughs gleefully each time. Sam yells at her to knock it off. (←Sam sees Riley’s humor as inappropriate)

Elias is annoyed that his favorite podcaster keeps getting pushback from the media for making jokes. They’re just jokes! (←Elias feels something is unfairly or nonsensically seen as inappropriate)

Trevor makes a joke about headscarves at his lab, and Hana and John get offended. Trevor gets annoyed.

Trevor: “Lighten up guys! My girlfriend wears a hijab. I’m pretty sure I know what’s appropriate or not!”

His lab mates shake their heads knowingly at Trevor’s crudeness and insensitivity.

Trevor is in a bad mood the rest of the day. (←Trevor disagrees with what his lab sees as inappropriate)

Above, humor or humor attempts recieved pushback. We'll be talking about why this does or doesn't happen throughout this chapter.
