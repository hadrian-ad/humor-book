Annotating humor with humor tells

We previously used examples of humor family words and said they were humor. We can use humor tells to describe what this humor looked like:

Absence. Trevor didn’t show many humor tells after the jump scare—because he wasn’t really feeling much safety! Trevor mostly shows inverse humor tell:

  • instead of loosening, he tightens
  • instead of feeling optimism, he feels dread
  • instead of flushing, he pales

(and so on)

While Trevor also shows a few humor tells, like touching self and social seeking, these are also anxiety tells or fear tells. Emotion tells often count for more than one emotion—which means social seeking can double count as both a humor tell and mistake-predominance tell.

In this case, we’d say Trevor has some humor in his emotional state after the jump scare—but that he’s mostly feeling discomfort or irritation.
