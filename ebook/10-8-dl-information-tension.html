A deeper look Information tension

As we increase our attention to each other’s emotional state, we increase the informational tension between them—forming a connection between them.

This human surface tension is what makes groups groups:

We can see the degrees of human surface tension any time we look at any group of people:

Zoe and Emma are walking down the campus quad, arguing about which dinosaur would make the best pet. (←high surface tension)

They pass someone relaxing on a bench. They register some basic things about that person’s emotional state. (←low surface tension)

We increase or decrease this informational tension by how much mutual attention we give each other:

Damien sees Xiaowei and Lina come over, and dreads having to talk to people.

Xiaowei: “Want to come sit with us?”

Damien gathers his lunch bag and sits with them. They include him in their conversation. As they explain some of their inside jokes to him, he starts to join the discussion more fully. (←Xiaowei and Lina increase surface tension with Damien)

Owen goes to office hours to speak to Justin. Owen pokes his head in.

Justin: “Come in!” *typing* “Hang on, I’ll be with you in just a second… Have a seat!”

Owen: *sits* (←lack of attention decreases surface tension)

Justin: *typing* *finishing email* *swivels* *looks at Owen* *puts hands together* “Okay. Now, what can I do for you?” (←attention increases surface tension)

Owen hates talking to professors. He always feels tongue-tied.

Owen goes up to the register. The barista is busy at the espresso machine. (←low surface tension)

Barista: *glancing over* (recognizing him) *smiles* “Hey! I’ll be right with you.”

Owen: *smiles* “Okay!” (←mutual attention increases surface tension)

The barista goes back to making her drink. (←reduction of mutual attention decreases surface tension)

A teacher corners Sam to complain. Sam nods and listens carefully, but Heather keeps going on and on. (←attention increases surface tension)

After about ten minutes, Sam briefly checks his phone, trying to signal to her that it’s time to wrap up the conversation. (←wandering attention decreases surface tension)

Heather keeps talking, and Sam listens patiently. (←attention increases surface tension)

Zoe is reading on the bus. Someone sits next to her. (←low human surface tension) She can feel him look over at her book. (←increased human surface tension)

Stranger: “Watcha reading?” (←stranger tries to increase surface tension)

Zoe: *not looking up* “Just an assignment.” (←Zoe tries to decrease surface tension)

Stranger: “Oh so you’re a student!” (←stranger tries to increase surface tension)

Zoe says something noncommittal and shifts to try to show she’s focusing on the book. (←Zoe tries to decrease surface tension)

"Grouping" is so normal that it's sometimes hard to even think about. Human surface tension is one way of drawing attention to the connection that groups us, how that connection happens, what it is, and how it changes over time.

These groupings are fluid and can quickly change over time—since we’re constantly changing which people we put effort into sharing and receiving information with.

Our ability to handle human surface tension is what enables us to form or disband groups. And while this skill is broader than just "humor," it's also essential to any skill around humor.

We can improve our awareness and control of human surface tension—which in turn enables connective and precise humor with others. That's because it gives us the nuanced information needed to shift mistakes and OKs on various topics to generate mutual humor.

Chapter summary

Evaluators radiate their evaluations to other evaluators. This creates a reactive network of evaluations. These emotion networks are the medium where humor happens.

  • We automatically join emotion networks whenever we're around others.
  • Emotion networks try to synchronize evaluations. People on emotion networks feel uncomfortable when they can't arrive at a shared evaluatory perspective.
  • Emotion networks can be nested within each other. These subnetworks try to stay synchronized on the sets of topics specific to that subnetwork.
