Humor and humor attempts have degrees of comparative fit to their emotional situation. We can estimate something's "fittedness" by its integration to a situation.

  • Fittedness focuses on how people do react to humor or humor attempts, not how someone thinks they "should" react.
  • We can estimate fittedness of humor or humor attempts by accounting for relevancy of topics, humor family words, and humor outcomes.
  • Higher fittedness increases emotional activity and leads to greater toleration on an emotion network.
