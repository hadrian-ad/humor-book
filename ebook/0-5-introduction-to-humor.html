Chapter 1

Humor

Are you ready to start? This book is about humor.

We can look at examples of what we mean by "humor:"

Zoe is nervous, but Emma cracks a joke.

Zoe relaxes and laughs. (←humor)

Owen is ruminating, but then his professor compliments his work.

It makes Owen beam. He laughs easily for a while after. (←humor)

Riley is riding her scooter on the sidewalk when she stumbles and falls. She starts to cry.

Sam lifts her up and she cries harder.

Sam says something that, despite herself, makes Riley giggle. (←humor)

Above, we're focusing on the smiling and laughing. ("Humor.")

Consider:

In this book, we'll focus on the role humor plays in dynamic experiences.

When Emma makes a joke, the point of interest isn't so much Emma's joke, but Zoe's change—from nervous to relaxed.
