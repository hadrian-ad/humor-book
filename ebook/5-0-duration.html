Chapter 5

Duration

Humor has a duration. It lasts for varying amounts of time: 

Minh and Trevor check their co-op score. 469.

Trevor: “69. Nice.”

Minh: “Heh.” (←short duration of humor)

- OR -

Minh: “Heheh.” (←slightly longer duration of humor)

Heather and Amber are taking a walk when Heather drops her phone. It falls down a grate, slipping perfectly through the cracks and disappearing.

Heather gives a loud short laugh of shock, and then becomes miserable. (←short duration of humor)

Amber laughs too, and laughs for the rest of the walk. (←long duration of humor) "I'm sorry, I'm sorry!" she says each time she laughs.

Owen: *telling joke* “… And that’s why they called him moo-shroom!”

Emma briefly laughs. (←short duration of humor) Zoe laughs for a full minute. (←long duration of humor)

Owen is about to cross the street but Zoe grabs him and pulls him back. A bus careens by, honking. Owen stands there, dumbfounded, knees shaking.

They both look at each other and laugh nervously. (←short duration of humor)

- OR -

They look at each other, and they giggle nervously for minutes. (←long duration of humor)

Emma and Owen have just seen Blood Soul Revenge in theaters. They laugh as they bus back to campus, in high spirits for hours. (←long duration of humor)

Trevor is about to ask Yasmin out for the first time. He sweats and his hands shake as he re-reads his text. He feels nauseous. What if she says no? Finally he hits send, and sits on his couch, ashen faced.

Yasmin: *texting* “Sure! I was hoping to hear from you!”

Trevor: *fist pumps* *beams* *laughs* *dances* *takes long ecstatic walk* (←long duration of humor)

Yasmin gets glasses for the first time and blinks in amazement. She can see the individual leaves on trees! She laughs and smiles all day, and can’t stop exclaiming to her coworkers. (←long duration of humor)

We’ll be looking at the duration of humor for the rest of this chapter.

Emotions and durations. All emotions have a duration to them—like how grief or irritation can last for a split second, a minute, an hour, a day, a month, and so on.

But if we're sad all day, that doesn't mean we're sobbing for twenty hours straight. Instead, the intensity of sadness increases and decreases over the course of the day. If our sadness crests into crying, it'll eventually pull back and continue at a lower intensity.

It’s the same for humor. When we feel humor all day, that doesn't mean we're laughing non-stop for twenty hours straight. Instead, the intensity of humor increases and decreases over the course of the day—and if it crests into laughing, it'll eventually pull back and continue at a lower intensity:

Any peak of humor will be bookended by less intense humor. To think about the duration of humor, we'll have pay close attention to the bookending moments of humor peaks.
