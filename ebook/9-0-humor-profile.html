Chapter 9

Humor Profile

In this chapter, we’ll talk about humor profiles.

Humor profiles are how someone express humor—the specific humor tells they tend to use when, at what intensity, and in what variation.

For instance:

If we watch five friends watch a funny video, they may all feel humor at the same time—but they won't express humor in the same way.

If we watch those five friends watch a hundred funny videos, we'll see that each friend is consistent in the individual way they express humor.

The individual way each person expresses humor is their humor profile.

Owen, Emma, and Zoe each express humor differently. These differences are their humor profile:

Owen, Emma, and Zoe overhear something that makes them smile.

Zoe gives a broad smile. (←Zoe’s humor profile is specific to her)

Emma sets her face as she tries to prevent her smile. (←Emma’s humor profile is specific to her)

Owen grins. (←Owen’s humor profile is specific to him)

Owen, Emma, and Zoe are doubled over at their table, laughing.

Zoe is slouched in her chair, one hand on her face, another on Emma’s shoulder. Zoe’s shoulders shake as she goes “haaa haaa haaa.” (←Zoe’s humor profile is specific to her)

Emma keeps her elbows on the table, dropping her head while she laughs, her feet linked together, going “aha aha aha.” (←Emma’s humor profile is specific to her)

Owen is flushed and has his hands clasped. He tends not to laugh very hard when he feels intense humor—he just smiles so hard his face hurts after. (←Owen’s humor profile is specific to him)

We'll be talking about humor profiles throughout this chapter.

Familiarity. The more familiar we are with someone's humor profile, the more accurately we can draw inferences from their humor.

For instance, Emma and Zoe have seen Owen smile tens of thousands of times, which lets them interpret small variations in his smile. But a stranger seeing Owen smile for the first time wouldn't himself have the same comparative basis as Emma and Zoe.

This means the stranger likely interprets Owen's emotional state with less accuracy and nuance than Emma and Zoe.
