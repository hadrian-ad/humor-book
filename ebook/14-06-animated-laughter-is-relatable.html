Laughter makes things emotionally vivid

We often find the most resonant parts of spectator entertainment to be scenes of characters laughing together:

Owen is watching Rick & Morty:

Testy rivals have been forced to collaborate and develop mutual respect for each other.

Zeep: (with sincerity) "If this works, drinks are on me."

Rick: "If drinks are on you, you're going to need a second mortgage." *leans in* *whispers* "I'm an alcoholic."

Zeep: *leans in* (shared disclosure) "Opium addict!"

Zeep and Rick share a cathartic, injured, bonding laugh. (←shared humor)

Owen laughs. For how aggressive and random this show can be, it's also sometimes surprisingly emotionally substantive.

Above, characters briefly balance mistakes and OKs, which allows them to feel mutual acceptance, comfort, connection, forgiveness, generosity, insight, reconciliation, release, revelation, relief, safety, and so on.

This variety of humor family words, shared between two people, makes the scene feel emotionally rich to Owen. The way the characters share an evaluatory transition feels meaningful, coherent, relatable, and integrated.

Consider:

Owen appreciates this scene not because it says anything profound about addiction or other subjects—but because the shared laughter between the characters creates a more engaging emotional story.

For instance, the emotional plot of this episode includes enemies becoming friends becoming enemies again. The intensity of this emotional plot partly relies on the quality of their shared humor in this moment.

Since it's hard for live-action characters to laugh convincingly on-screen, let alone laugh together—these emotionally resonant scenes tend to either be flat or absent from live-action media.
