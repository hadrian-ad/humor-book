Recapitulation of Volume I

This book detailed an analytical model of emotions, with an emphasis on humor. We learned to notice and verbalize humor dynamics in interactions and information processing.

  • Humor is an emotion that occurs in discoverable patterns
  • Humor is part of how we communicate information-assessments with ourselves and others.
  • Humor refers to a broad set of possible configurations of behavior, which isn't limited to just smiling and laughing.
  • Humor is acceptance, connections, insight, joy, and so on.
  • Humor is the the mechanism of concordant influence.
  • Humor serves as a the emotional orientation of our lives.

This is one (of many possible) ways to talk, in an organized system, about positive emotions in our lives.
