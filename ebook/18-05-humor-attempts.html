Humor attempts

Velocity is one quality of an intention to generate humor. In this book, we'll call intentions to generate humor humor attempts.

consider:

Whenever we try to make someone show humor tells, push someone into humor tripwires, feel humor family words—we're making a humor attempt.

A humor attempt is broader than a joke. A humor attempt includes everything—not just the words of a joke, but also the intonation, pacing, posture, facial expression, broader situation, and so on.

That means the joke itself would just be part of the humor attempt.

So if a hundred people tell the same joke, they're using one joke but making a hundred different humor attempts—since each person would be:

  • talking to a different person
  • using a different inflection
  • have a different relation to the joke
  • speaking at different times of day

(and so on)

Similarly, humor attempts are anything that intends someone to feel humor. So bringing our partner tea can a humor attempt—if our intention is for them to smile or loosen and ease tension and experience belonging, comfort, delight, and so on.

If our partner smiles, it's a successful humor attempt—if they frown with disappointment, it's an unsuccessful humor attempt. Regardless of outcome, it's still a humor attempt if we intended humor.

Consider:

Throughout this book, we'll use "humor attempts" to talk about the broad range of things that intend someone to feel humor.

This includes things that push people toward humor outcomes. When Sam puts on his jacket to go with Jennifer, or Zoe massages Emma's shoulders—they're making humor attempts! Their goal is for Jennifer and Emma to reach humor tripwires.

Relation. "Humor attempt" has less ambiguity than words like "joke" or "insult". Someone can intend grief from a joke, just like they can intend laughter from an insult.

By descripting intention first, we can better characterize something without worrying about describing it with a genre word. So when the tutor says "this is terrible," we can simply call it a humor attempt without worrying too much about words like insults or jokes.

More importantly, "humor attempts" gives us a way to talk collectively about everything we do to generate smiles or laughs. This includes the ones we don't really have names for—like bringing tea, massaging shoulders, giving sincere compliments, giving sincere criticism, and so on.
