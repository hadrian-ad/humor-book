Humor bushes are individualized

Everyone in Heather’s class will have a different size humor bush, since each student feels a different amount of humor:

  • Xiaowei feels the content is unmanageable, and so often feels confusion and failure instead of humor. (Less humor overall, lower opinion)
  • Lina feels the material is difficult but manageable, and frequently feels accomplishment and insight. (More humor overall, higher opinion)
  • Santiago feels the content is tragically easy and often feels boredom and waste. (Less humor overall, lower opinion)

Even though they’re all seeing the same content, their opinion of the class varies based on their humor bush:

Xiaowei and Santiago still think positively of Heather’s class—but don’t think of the class as positively as Lina does, who most often feels humor.

Consider:

We can also compare different periods of life. We may have a period of time with a partner we think back on fondly, which was followed by a period of trouble. We have a large humor bush with one set of memories, and a smaller humor bush with another—and our overall opinion depends on how we segment our memories.
