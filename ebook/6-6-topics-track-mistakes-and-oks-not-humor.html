Topics track mistakes and OKs, not humor

Topics track mistakes and OKs. These evaluations can generate humor, but don't always. 

Some of our topics may never generate humor at all—like how the topic ‘houseplant’ might never produce any humor our entire lives: 

But once we estimate the size of the mistake and OK on a topic, we have the information we need to try to generate humor from them.

For instance, since ‘houseplant’ has a large OK, we could try using a large mistake to generate humor. We could: 

  • pretend it's a therapist and talk to it
  • focus on it to try to see it in new light
  • pretend our houseplant is a reincarnated white pine
  • give it a name like Augusta or Lord Grom
  • take many pictures of it wearing a scarf and share them with a friend

(and so on)

These would be larger mistakes that may match the large OK—and so generate humor.

Effort. Sometimes it doesn't seem to take effort of our own to feel humor—like when we watch comedic movies, and feeling humor is almost automatically. We don't feel involved in generating our own humor.

Things are a bit different with something like a houseplant—since involving a houseplant in feeling humor isn't automatic, but often takes effort and initiative.

But once we can generate humor from something like a houseplant, we can generate humor anywhere, on any topic—since any topic can have balanced mistakes and OKs.
