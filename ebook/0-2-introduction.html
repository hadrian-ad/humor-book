Welcome!

We all laugh at jokes and comedies—sometimes, anyway.

But let's look beyond those things. Have you ever:

  • put on a shirt hot from the dryer (and laughed?)
  • biked downhill (and laughed?)
  • saw your favorite coworker (and laughed?)
  • heard someone make a great point (and laughed?)
  • came to a scenic overlook (and laughed?)
  • assessed bad news (and laughed?)
  • teased or got teased (and laughed?)
  • got a really good hug (and laughed?)
  • had lots of snot from crying (and laughed?)

(and so on)

Once it's pointed out, it's obvious we smile and laugh after more than just jokes and comedies. What's going on here?

In this book, we'll look at the Big Idea that explains all instances of smiling and laughing—jokes as well as hugs

We'll find explanations that are powerful, and have relevance to our lives, goals, and dreams.

Consider:

The present-day word "dream" has uncertain etymology—but the Old English dream meant joy, mirth, and noisy merriment (music, celebrations etc).

This is one of a number of interesting and often surprising elements of humor hiding in our life and history—which we'll be exploring as we go through this book.

While summarizing an explanation can be done quickly, actually learning them takes longer. This book is isn't a summary—it's an in-depth tool for developing skills and useful perspectives.

We'll develop these through 27 carefully-sequenced chapters, which use more than 600 examples and 300 figures—making this a practical course for serious instruction on humor.

In many ways, the study of humor is the root of all good knowledge. This is something that will be clear as we progress through the book.

Not. There are a few things this book is not. It's not a scholarly book—this book will not catch you up on how various academic disciplines have thought about humor.

That means this also isn't a book about neurochemistry, computational biology, large language models, literary history, business models for comedy, or other approaches one might take to humor.

Instead, this is a book of field research—and of what's knowable about humor just by observation and ordered thinking.
