Charm attempts often have high-velocity

We saw before how low-velocity comments don't always reach humor outcomes in complicated situations.

That's why charm often involves high-velocity toward humor outcomes:

Tutor: *finishes reading Zoe’s grad school application essay* *sighs* “This is pretty terrible.” (←tutor rushes toward outcomes like controlling the situation, opening new topics of conversation, acceptably criticizing, and so on)

Zoe: *cathartic laugh* (←tutor rushes Zoe into humor outcome tripwires) “Really? Oh no.”

- OR -

Tutor: *finishes reading Zoe’s grad school application essay* “Um. So this is… okay. There were things I liked about it. But we should maybe talk about your approach.” (←tutor doesn't rush toward humor outcomes like controlling the situation, opening new topics of conversation, acceptably criticizing, and so on)

Zoe: *confused* “So I’m on the right track?” (←Zoe doesn’t reach any humor outcomes)

If the tutor used less velocity, Zoe may have defended those tripwires and not have felt fitted humor. The “nicer” version may have been worse charm!
