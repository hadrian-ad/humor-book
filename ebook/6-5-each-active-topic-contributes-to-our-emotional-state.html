Each active topic contributes to our emotional state

When a topic is active, it generates emotions into our emotional state:

A. Seventh grade Emma remembers her old teacher Ms. Gardener. She feels very calm, thinking about her. (←topic 'Ms. Gardener' is active, generating calmness into Emma's emotional state)

B. As adults, Emma and Zoe reflect on Ms. Gardener and make inferences about her personality, laughing as they do. (←topic 'Ms. Gardener' is active, generating humor into Emma's emotional state)

We can have multiple topics active at once. All our active topics together make up our emotional state:

So if one topic generates humor, and another generates mystery, then we can feel humor and mystery simultaneously when both are activated together:

Emma and Zoe are watching Fired Fireman 4: Moonshot.

A. Chief is looking at his map as he stomps around Mars. “I could have sworn this was the way to the Mars Bazaar,” he mutters, turning the map upside down and tilting his helmet to look at it. (← activates topic ‘quest to buy a space scarf'↔︎Emma and Zoe feel humor)

B. He takes a step and falls down a hole. As the dust settles, he looks up at the ancient, dark statues looking down at a mysterious futuristic pod encased in ancient, frozen water. (←activates topic ‘mysterious frozen chamber on Mars’↔︎Emma and Zoe feel mystery)

C. “Excuse me!” Chief calls out. “I need some directions!” (←both topics are active↔︎Emma and Zoe humor and mystery)
