In each of these relationships, there’s a humor dress code for specific instances (“is this specific humor attempt fitted?”) as well as a humor dress code for humor in general. (“Is this overall amount of humor fitted?”)

To find humor bush dress codes, we take our humor outcomes one-by-one. For each one, we’d think about what degree of characterization would be fitted or unfitted.

For instance:

We’d ask “to what degree should we characterize our relationship with our doctor (or neighbor, lover, trainer, or so on) with a humor outcome? Should we <never/occasionally/often/always> symbolize close ties?"

So with our life partner we may think we should always symbolize close ties. With our neighbor, just often symbolize close ties. With our doctor, just occasionally symbolize close ties. This characterization of fitted frequency helps us think about the fittedness of humor bushes.
