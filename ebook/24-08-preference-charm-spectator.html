We have a long-term preference for spectator charm.

We have a long-term preference for charming people in our interpersonal interactions—we have a long-term preference for charming things in spectator entertainment too.

When we think back to our favorite entertainment over the years, the things that come to mind are usually things with lots of charm to them:

For instance, we might have fond memory of media about:

  • sponges and squids that deal with work and boredom in different ways

  • a space crew of mercenaries and passengers
  • washed up and depressed animals past the peak of their careers

(and so on)

It was the charm mixed into the comedy that kept these things meaningful to us over longer periods of time—for the same reason it's the charm that makes us remember friends or strangers years later.
