When I went to university for the first time, I struggled to understand the value of much of what I encountered. I left to read full-time for a few years, which was a formative period of trying to make sense of things.

In some Islamic traditions, books don't have sources so much as "nutrients"—and in that spirit I might list some of the more important books I read. These nutrients include influence of style, presentation, sequence, education, and worldview.

Please note that I did not literature review of humor or analagous terms, as that was outside the scope and purpose of this project.

Nutrients

D.H. Lawrence, whose poetic work is focused on a core of vivacity and change.

Rumi, whose uses beautiful metaphors and inversions to convey framings—and grounds purpose in a kind of ecstatic humor.

Cartoon History of the Universe series, which uses such fitted humor to interpret history they stick hard.

Quicksilver, a book of light density and ecstatic construction.
