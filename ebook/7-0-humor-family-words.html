Chapter 7

Humor Family Words

In this chapter, we’ll look at humor family words. These are contextual variations of the word humor: 

Humor family words are moments where we feel humor:

Justin and Heather are mountain biking when Justin flips on a root and tumbles down a small hill. “Oh my god! Justin! Are you okay?” Heather calls, jumping off her bike and scrambling down to the body and bike below.

Justin stirs and sits up. “Oof,” he says, adjusting his helmet shakily. “I’m fine!”

They look at each other and start giggling with relief. (←mistake is OK↔︎realization, relief, release, safety↔︎humor)

We can describe Heather and Justin’s humor as humor—but also as humor family words, like relief, realization, safety, and so on.

The generic word "humor" by itself doesn't imply any context:

Above, the mistake and OK could be related to any situation. When we want to give more situational context, we replace humor with a humor family word—like relief:

Consider:

We can use humor family words to talk about humor with narrative clarity—since humor family words like acceptance, connection, success, and so on evoke more distinctive methods and situations than “humor” does by itself.
