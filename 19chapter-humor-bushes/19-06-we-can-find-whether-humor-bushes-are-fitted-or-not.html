Humor bushes are fitted by need for concord

Humor bushes are fitted within different ranges for any pair of people—so not just between us and our romantic partner and our students, but also between us and our:

  • corrections officer
  • food vendor
  • lifting buddy
  • professor we’re working for
  • adolescent child’s romantic partner
  • social media audience
  • medical specialist
  • homeless patron

(and so on)

In each of these relationships, there’s both a humor dress code for specific instances—“is this specific humor attempt fitted?”—as well as a humor dress code for humor in general—“is this overall amount of humor fitted?”

To find humor bush dress codes, we take our humor outcomes one-by-one. For each one, we’d think about what degree of characterization would be fitted or unfitted.

For instance:

We’d ask “to what degree should we characterize our relationship with our doctor (or neighbor, lover, trainer, or so on) with a humor outcome? Should we <never/occasionally/often/always> symbolize close ties?"

So with our life partner we may think we should always symbolize close ties. With our neighbor, just often symbolize close ties. With our doctor, just occasionally symbolize close ties. This characterization of fitted frequency helps us think about the fittedness of humor bushes.

Then we’d go through other humor outcomes, like acceptably criticize, reveal personal views, and so on. As we go through each humor outcome, we’ll arrive at an intuitive sense of what would be too much or too little.

Standards. The humor dress code is set by our need for efficiency and cohesion—which is true for individual moments of humor as well as for collective moments of humor.

We can use efficiency and cohesion to think about the humor dress code for humor bushes. How much efficiency and cohesion do we need with our coworkers or clients—not just in specific moments, but overall? How would the humor bush dress code change when someone stops being our client, and we just become friends?

We can often think about the humor dress code of groups or places. We can ask: “how cohesive and effective does a tour bus have to be?”—or a classroom, waiting room, or so on. This can help us estimate the upper and lower ranges for the dress code of humor bushes.
