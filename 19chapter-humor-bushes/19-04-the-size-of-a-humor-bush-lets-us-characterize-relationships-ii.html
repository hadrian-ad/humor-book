13. Evaluatory information

Different relationships have different sizes of humor bushes. For instance, Trevor and his twelve-year-old nephew Sebastian have a small humor bush:

Since they only have a small humor bush, we’d characterize their overall relationship as having some rapport, low-ish tension, control of the situation mostly in the hands of the one with more status, some petty conflict, and so on.

Consider:

This isn’t necessarily what happens moment-to-moment. Sometimes Trevor and Sebastian mutually feel intense humor. We’d use stronger intensities of humor outcomes to characterize them in that particular moment—“in this moment, they feel strong rapport.”

But if they only occasionally feel intense mutual humor around each other, we’d characterize their overall relationship with low-intensity humor outcomes.

By contrast, Emma and Zoe have a large humor bush:

In this case, we’d characterize their overall relationship as having full rapport, no tension, able to mutually control situations, never in conflict, and so on.
