The Humor Book

Whoever feels humor feels a humor outcome

The person who feels humor is the person who experiences humor outcomes. That's why it’s important to track who is really feeling humor:

Heather: *hands on hips* “Xiaowei, when are you going to start looking for a job?”

Xiaowei: *playing a game* (listlessly) “When my shit turns blue.”

Heather: *throws hands up* (←Heather does not feel anyone avoided conflict↔︎no humor)

Xiaowei: *laughs* (←Xiaowei feels he avoids conflict↔︎humor)

Emma stops by Max’s place and goes to look at his cockatiel. The bird imitates Emma’s vocal cadence perfectly. “Hello?” it says.

Emma laughs, and says: “You’re a moron you know, but that’s why I like you.” (←Emma symbolizes close ties with cockatiel↔︎humor)

The cockatiel looks at her. (←Cockatiel does not symbolize close ties↔︎no humor)

The bird didn't feel humor—Emma did. So it's Emma who symbolizes close ties with the cockatiel—not the cockatiel with Emma. The same thing happens when we replace the bird with a human:

Emma stops by Max’s place. Max imitates Emma’s vocal cadence perfectly. “Hello?” he says.

Emma laughs, and says: “You’re a moron, you know, but that’s why I like you.” (←Emma symbolizes close ties to Max↔︎humor)

Max looks at her. (←Max does not symbolize close ties to Emma↔︎no humor)

Max didn't feel humor humor—Emma did. So it's Emma who symbolized close ties with Max—something that wasn’t reciprocated at that particular moment by Max, for whatever reason.
