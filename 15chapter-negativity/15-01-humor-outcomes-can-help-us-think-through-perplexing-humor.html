The Humor Book

Humor outcomes describe motivation

To make sense of perplexing situations involving humor, we can avoid thinking about "humor"—and think about humor outcomes instead:

Heather: *hands on hips* “Xiaowei, when are you going to start looking for a job?”

Xiaowei: *playing a game* (listlessly) “When my shit turns blue.”

Heather: *throws hands up* (←Heather feels Xiaowei is being flippant)

Xiaowei: *laughs* (←mistakes and OKs balance↔︎humor)

Why did Xiaowei laugh? We might say he:

  • avoided conflict (about how he was spending time)
  • handled a complaint (by shutting down a conversation)
  • revealed a personal view (that he doesn’t want to get a job)
  • supported an argument (that this isn’t a good time for a conversation)
  • eased tensions (with himself)
  • controlled a situation (by ending a conversation)

(and so on)

Xiaowei laughed because he purposefully reached or stumbled into humor outcomes, like avoiding conflict or controlling a situation. This puts his behavior into an understandable framing.

Consider:

Describing Xiaowei’s humor with humor outcomes doesn’t mean he was effective; did something well; that others shouldn’t describe him as rude; or so on.

Instead, we’re trying to avoid what can sometimes be our first impression: that he was being erratic; that his behavior was inscrutable; that humor is fundamentally inexplicable; or so on.

Heather didn’t feel humor, because she didn't feel Xiaowei avoided conflict—she felt Xiaowei created conflict.

Topics and humor outcomes. Humor outcomes describe something happening to a specific topic. So we don't achieve catharsis on everything all at once when we laugh—instead, just the topic that generates humor achieves catharsis.

We can choose between different humor outcomes depending on the topic that generates humor. When Heather and Xiaowei have a conversation about a job, some of his active topics might be:

  • ‘Heather’s reaction’
  • ‘this conversation’
  • ‘Xiaowei’s overall life’

If we intuitively feel Xiaowei felt he avoided conflict, controlled a situation, and handled a complaint, we can loosely correspond each one to their most contextually relevant topic:

  • ‘Heather’s reaction’→Xiaowei feels he avoided conflict (with his mother)
  • ‘this conversation’→Xiaowei feels he controlled the situation (the conversation)
  • ‘Xiaowei’s overall life’→Xiaowei feels he handled a complaint (about his life)
