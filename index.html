This book is about humor.

Before looking at humor more specifically, we can look at some examples of the kinds of things we'll be looking at:

Zoe is nervous, but Emma cracks a joke.

Zoe laughs and becomes more comfortable. (←humor)

Owen is ruminating, but then his professor compliments his work.

It makes Owen beam, and he laughs easily for a while after. (←humor)

Riley is playing on the sidewalk when she trips and skims her knee. She starts to cry.

Sam says something that makes Riley giggle, despite herself. (←humor)

Above, we're focusing on the smiling and laughing—and their role in dynamic experiences.

Consider:

When Emma cracks a joke, the point of interest isn't so much Emma's joke, but Zoe's change from nervous to comfortable. This sort of dynamic is the focus of this book.

These dynamics around humor are intimately familiar. Depsite this, we often find them hard to think through. Why are things funny? What do smiles mean?

In this book, we'll learn to describe and understand these experiences around smiling and laughing. This will make us more capable in our ability to generate and understand smiles and laughs—and let us uncover some surprising, important insights.

Everywhere. Everyone has a lifetime of experiences with humor. This book takes a careful look at those experiences, and gives catalytic insight to organize those experiences in a coherent system.

This means taking into account the humor that happens all across our lives—when aroused, bitter, confused, frustrated, joyful, sad, triumphant, and so on. Humor in Various Moods and Meters explains this variety through the same system.
