Humor in Various Moods and Meters

An Introduction to Humor Dynamics

Welcome!

This book is an introduction to humor dynamics—one of the most important subjects in the world, and also one of the least studied.

Zoe is nervous, but Emma cracks a joke. Zoe laughs and becomes more comfortable. (←humor dynamics)

Owen is ruminating, but then his professor compliments his work. It makes Owen beam, and he laughs easily for a while after. (←humor dynamics)

Jessica drops something and laughs. Then, overwhelmed, she starts to cry.

Emma talks with her until Jessica wipes her eyes and gives a small laugh. (←humor dynamics)

Above, the smiles and laughs didn't happen in isolation. Instead, they're part of dynamic experiences.

Consider:

When Emma cracks a joke, the point of interest isn't so much Emma's joke, but Zoe's change from nervous to comfortable. This sort of dynamic is the focus of this book.

Despite how familiar these dynamics are, they're often outside our ability to talk about. It's a bit like trying to explain why we like a song—something we experience effortlessly is hard to convey.

In this book, we'll learn to describe and understand these experiences. This will make us more capable in our ability to generate and understand smiles and laughs—and let us uncover some surprising, important insights.

Everyone has a lifetime of experiences with humor. This book takes a careful look at those experiences, to see what insight we can gain about the world.

Everywhere. Humor can happen all across our lives—when aroused, bitter, confused, frustrated, joyful, sad, triumphant, and so on. Humor in Various Moods and Meters explains this variety through the same system.
