The Humor Book

Welcome!

In this book, we'll answer all kinds of questions about humor—including the big ones, like "what is humor? And how does it work?"

By the end, we'll easily be able to answer questions like: "why did I find that funny?" or "how could someone possibly laugh at that?!"

 

We'll learn practical things that we'll use every day. We'll dive right in!

Q. What is The Humor Book?

A. The Humor Book is a step-by-step explanation of humor.

Q. So it's about jokes?

A. Not specifically—this book is about the when and why of smiling and laughing. That's inclusive to jokes—but it's also inclusive to everything else as well!

While we've laughed at jokes before, we've also laughed at insults, compliments, vegetables, hugs, success, failure, cute things, gross things, and so on.

We'll want to explain the presence or absence of laughter in all of these cases—for jokes as well as for hugs.

Q. Can humor really be taught?

A. Yes! There are many myths about humor—like that humor is abstract, unknowable, undefinable, unteachable, and so on. None of those are true! Usually, those statements describe the speaker more than they describe humor: "humor is unteachable (by me)."

Q. Who is this book on humor for?

A. Learning about humor is both useful and interesting to all kinds of people. This book helps:

  • architects and interior designers think about why some designs lead to more smiles over time than others
  • facilitators and service workers better handle conflict and create compromise
  • musicians and painters make conscious creative choices leading to smiles
  • parents and children better understand each other
  • performers and public speakers find new perspectives on their audience
  • researchers and historians have an analytical model to uniformly describe and interpret laughter and smiling
  • romantic partners and family members more broadly generate laughter in people they care about
  • teachers and instructors gain insights about their class and methods
  • transplants and transfers better form connections with strangers, new neighbors, and new coworkers

(and so on)

If any of the skills and outcomes above interest you, this book is for you.
