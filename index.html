Humor in Various Moods and Meters

An Introduction to Humor Dynamics

Welcome!

This book is an introduction to humor dynamics. While "humor dynamics" is often a new term to us, these dynamics are as familiar to us as breathing:

  • We're nervous—but someone cracks a joke and makes us laugh.
  • A stranger compliments us—and we smile and laugh easily for an hour after.
  • We talk to someone and start crying—but eventually we wipe our eyes and smile.

These moments of smiling and laughing are inflection points that define outcomes and relationships—what this book refers to as humor dynamics.

But while we universally notice these dynamics, we don't always know how to talk about them—let alone think through them in an organized way.

For instance:

Let's say we laugh because we're told a joke—and later laugh because we're complimented. Are these moments of laughing different, or similar?

If we're unsure of the relationship between jokes, compliments, and laughs, we have some careful thinking to do to make sense of what, exactly, is going on here.

One part of this is noticing where we actually laugh. Until it's pointed out, many of us haven't noticed that we laugh when complimented—or that, for the vast majority of us, we make others laugh more often through compliments than we do through jokes.

We can break things down a bit. In this book, humor refers to smiling and laughing. "Humor" is something that happens, or is felt:

Yasmin puts on her glasses for the first time. She can see leaves! Clouds have texture! She beams and laughs. (←humor)

Humor dynamics refers to the process that determines when and why we smile and laugh—and what that does in situations.

Yasmin puts on her glasses for the first time. She can see leaves! Clouds have texture! She beams and laughs. (←why does Yasmin laugh? Why does she stop laughing? Why did she laugh as hard as she did, and not harder? Why would someone laugh seeing Yasmin laugh? Why would she eventually not laugh when putting her glasses on? What can we infer about Yasmin from this laugh? What does this humor "mean"? And so on)

We'll be taking a systemic approach to humor, taking its dynamics are fundamentally understandable. We'll be learning about these dynamics step-by-step throughout this book.

Consider:

Even though we all smile and laugh, a formal study of humor is still important! A good analogy is to geometry. Rectangles and triangles are familiar, intuitive shapes—which is true even as a good geometry class opens up all kinds of practical skills by guiding us through explicit ideas about angles and area.

Extending the metaphor, we may also find that, while we can intuitively work through rectangles well enough, a lack of formal geometry leaves us unequipped to deal with quadrilaterals—and we sometimes just don't think about them at all because of that.

We'll find something similar with humor, where smiling and laughter are in many ways intuitive—which can still be true even as a good formal study opens up all kinds of useful and interesting things, often that take us deeper into things than we suspected at the outset.

For many of us, this will be a fresh approach to smiles and laughter, giving us increased insight and ability—which will have ramifications all across our lives.

Alchemy of Happiness. Humor dynamics are the most important, least studied subject in the world. It's likely you were given several math textbooks in high school—but zero on humor!

It's left many of us with a puzzling relation to our experiences. We grew up describing our sense of humor as weird, or were hurt by being mocked. We felt compelled to do things to try to make others laugh—we were drawn to places that make us smile—we laughed with everyone else even when someone was being harmed—we laughed when we cried and sometimes laughed when others cried.

More fundamentally, we felt connected or alienated based on the smiles and laughs around us, but never really clearly understood this dynamic due to apparent incoherence. This book untangles our experiences so that we can make sense of all this, and apply that understanding elegantly and productively.

For humor, this might have led to give-up ideas that we wouldn't tolerate elsewhere ("humor is unknowable") or ideas from entertainers or literary theorists that have limited relevance to our day-to-day life.

This book provides a survey of the humor dynamics that happen in daily life, between people—like how and why humor is involved in making and keeping friends, and how we can understand how people are experiencing the world around them.
