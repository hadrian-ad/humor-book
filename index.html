Humor

This book is about humor. We can look at examples of what we mean by "humor:"

Most people take a class on geometry—but few people take a class on smiling and laughing!

-->

Zoe is nervous, but Emma cracks a joke.

Zoe laughs and becomes more comfortable. (←humor)

Owen is ruminating, but then his professor compliments his work.

It makes Owen beam, and he laughs easily for a while after. (←humor)

Riley is riding her scooter on the sidewalk when she falls. She starts to cry.

Sam says something that makes Riley giggle, despite herself. (←humor)

Above, we're focusing on the smiling and laughing. ("Humor.") We're also focusing on the role humor plays in dynamic experiences.

When Emma cracks a joke, the point of interest isn't so much Emma's joke, but Zoe's change from nervous to comfortable.

Over the course of this book, we'll be becoming intimitately familiar with humor—and improve significantly in our ability to describe and create humor.
