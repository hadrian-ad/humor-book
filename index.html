Humor in Various Moods and Meters

An Introduction to Humor Dynamics

Welcome!

This book is an introduction to humor dynamics. While this is often a new term, the dynamics of humor are as familiar to us as breathing:

  • We're nervous, but someone cracks a joke and makes us laugh.
  • A stranger compliments us and we smile and laugh easily for an hour after.
  • We're crying as we talk to someone, but eventually we wipe our eyes and smile.

We universally experience these moments as inflection points that define outcomes and relationships—what this book refers to as humor dynamics.

But while we universally notice these dynamics, we don't always know exactly how to talk about them—let alone think through them in an organized way.

For instance:

Here's an example of our disorganization: if we smile because we hear a joke, and smile because we hear a compliment: are these moments of smiling and laughing different, or similar?

We sometimes put laughs that stem from "jokes" into their own bin, and don't think much about (or ignore) the laughs everywhere else. Until it's pointed out, many of us don't even notice that people laugh when praised!

As we'll see, there's good reason to treat all laughing and smiling as under the same umbrella, without worrying about whether jokes are involved or not. We'll arrive at this conclusion through some organized thinking.

We can break things down a bit. In this book, humor refers to smiling and laughing. It's something that happens, or is felt.

Humor dynamics refers to the process that determines when and why we smile and laugh—and what that does in situations.

We can learn about these dynamics, which gives us insight and capability. It's also very interesting! In some ways, humor dynamics are the most important, and least studied subject in the world.

Beginnings. Even though this book starts "at the beginning," it's not remedial instruction in humor. Instead, this book supplies catalytic insight to our lifetime of observations and experiences with humor.

An analogy is to geometry. Like smiles and laughs, we've spent our life around rectangles and triangles—which in some ways are simple, intuitive shapes.

Even so, there are things to learn about them, and maybe more than we suspected at first—and learning about angles and area opens up all kinds of practical skills. We'll find that something similar happens with humor dynamics.
