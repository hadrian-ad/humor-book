The Humor Book

It's optional whether we censure unfitted humor

While we can censure a humor attempt when it’s below dress code, we don’t have to. There's often no reason to censure unfitted humor—like if everyone is still synchronized after unfitted humor:

Owen: “What if we…” *laughs* “…shit all over the classroom?” *laughs harder* (←unfitted humor)

Emma: *laughs* “Class, I’ve never read…” *laughs* “…such mush… before…” (←unfitted humor)

Owen: *crying from laughter* “Such slippery prose…” (←unfitted humor)

Owen and Emma: *whoop with laughter* (←unfitted humor)

Owen: “Think fast, Zo!” *flicks a sunflower seed at her* (←unfitted humor attempt)

Zoe: “Return fire! Fire the catapults!” *loads spoon with grape* (←unfitted humor attempt)

Owen can make an accurate emotional forecast of how Zoe will react when he flicks things at her. This lets him confidently make this unfitted humor attempt with her.

Consider:

By contrast, Owen couldn't forecast how a stranger would react if he flicked things at them—which is why Owen is more likely to have unfitted humor with a friend like Zoe than with a stranger.

Being below dress code just means that if we censure it, others will find that censure justified:

Zoe hugs a stranger.

Stranger: "Um." (←stranger chooses to censure Emma)

- OR -

Stranger: *smiles* “Thanks! I needed that." (←stranger doesn’t choose to censure Zoe)

Emma: “Trevor was dressed like a carrot today.” (←unfitted humor attempt)

Zoe: (warningly) “Emma.” *laughs* (←Zoe chooses to censure Emma)

- OR -

Zoe: “Oh my gosh! You’re right!” *laughs* (←Zoe doesn’t choose to censure Emma)

Tucker pokes his head out of his office to talk to Amber.

Tucker: “Okay. Pun-time. Here we go: I’m a big fan of whiteboards. I find them totally re-markable!”

Amber: “Ha ha Tucker, not bad, but you know I’m pretty busy.” (←Amber chooses to censure Tucker by expressing less humor as well as minor exasperation)

Tucker: “Well, keep at ‘em!” *goes back into office* (←Tucker sees censure as justified)

Yasmin listens to the exchange. Tucker shouldn’t bother Amber like that during work. (←Yasmin sees Amber’s censure as justified)

- OR -

Amber: “Hah. That’s pretty good, Tucker.” (←Amber chooses not to censure Tucker)

Tucker: *pleased* “Well, keep at ‘em!” *goes back into office.*

Yasmin listens to the exchange. Tucker is so annoying. (←Yasmin would have seen censure as justified)

Jasper is doing stand-up.

Jasper: “Okay, okay. So here’s another joke. Let’s see. So there are two recovering alcoholics, right? And they sit down. And they’re like—let’s get our act together and finally be rock stars, like we always wanted. So they start trying to write a song. But they can’t get past the first two bars!”

The audience guffaws, and Sam laughs too—nothing like a joke in slightly bad taste.

As the laughter dies down, the audience catches the tail end of a groan. It causes people to chuckle, and Jasper raises his hands in good-natured self-defense. (←someone censures an unfitted humor attempt, and people support the censurer)

- OR -

The audience guffaws, and Sam laughs too—nothing like a joke in slightly bad taste. (←no one bothers to censure an unfitted humor attempt)

Above, Sam doesn’t bother to censure an unfitted humor attempt himself. But if someone censures it, Sam will see their censure as justified.

Consider:

That doesn’t mean Sam would approve of censure of any strength. Instead, Sam will see censure as justified in proportion to how unfitted it was.

Since Jasper’s humor attempt wasn’t particularly unfitted, Sam will see a good-natured groan as justified censure—but will see yelling at the stage as unjustified censure.

Normalcy. Desynchronization and resynchronization is a normal part of being around others, because censure and dress codes are how we group and collaborate:

Emma: “Where do you want to eat? The Loaded Gyro?”

Zoe: *exaggerated grimace* (←Zoe censures Emma)

Owen: (compromising) “How about Humble Pie?”

Emma: *lights up* “Let’s do it!” (←emotion network resynchronizes and agrees on a course of action)

Above, Zoe’s censure of Emma (exaggerated grimace) wasn’t received as harsh—it was just part of a collaborative, back-and-forth process that allowed evaluations to be shared and reacted to. This allowed a group to reach a consensus in just a few seconds.

Censure is just a normal part of how conversations and groups work—and only occasionally does the same mechanism get scaled up to become confrontational.
