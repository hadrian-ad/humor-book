The Humor Book

Fittedness is measured against the dress code

Similar things can be fitted or unfitted based on the situation's humor dress code:

Zoe is browsing through the bookstore, and finds a book called Ferdydurke. (←humor attempt with low fittedness and low dress code↔︎fitted)

She smiles.

- OR -

Owen is writing an email to Justin. For fun, in the subject lines he writes “He cries FERDYDURKE!” (←humor attempt with low fittedness and high dress code↔︎unfitted)

Justin is amused and annoyed. Why doesn’t anyone ever read the email etiquette guidelines on the syllabus?

It’s the end of the day. Amber is packing up to go home, and gives a sigh of relief that the day’s over. Tucker pokes his head into her office unannounced.

Tucker: “Okay. Pun-time. Here we go: I’m a big fan of whiteboards. I find them totally re-markable!” (←humor attempt with low fittedness and low dress code↔︎fitted)

- OR -

Amber is busy during peak hours. Tucker pokes his head into her office unannounced. Amber looks up, feeling a bit frazzled by everything.

Tucker: “Okay. Pun-time. Here we go: I’m a big fan of whiteboards. I find them totally re-markable!” (←humor attempt with low fittedness and high dress code↔︎unfitted)

It’s the end of the day. Amber is packing up to go home, and gives a sigh of relief that the day’s over. Tucker pokes his head into her office unannounced.

Tucker: “Amber, great work with setting up that meeting today. It was flawless. I don’t know what we’d do without you.” (←humor attempt with high fittedness and low dress code↔︎fitted)

- OR -

Amber is busy during peak hours. Tucker pokes his head into her office unannounced. Amber looks up, feeling a bit frazzled by everything.

Tucker: “Amber, great work with setting up that meeting today. It was flawless. I don’t know what we’d do without you.” (←humor attempt with high fittedness and high dress code↔︎fitted)

Tucker’s pun had a low fittedness, since it was on irrelevant topics and wasn’t trying to reach humor family words. By contrast, Tucker’s affirmation had a high fittedness, since it was on relevant topics and was trying to make Amber feel appreciation, connection, belonging, delight, and so on.

Because of this high fittedness, the affirmation fit the emotional situation whether or not there was a high or low dress code.

Formation. Dress codes are so omnipresent that they can be hard to notice. But dress codes are always around, everywhere—which is why we:

  • encounter awkward pauses around the dinner table
  • exchange looks with each other when the teacher makes an uncomfortable joke
  • find random pointless stories from our spouse fun, but random pointless stories from the guy in the line in front of us annoying
  • cringe when watching an uncomfortable scene in a comedy
  • make eye contact with others when someone is weird in public
  • share looks with passengers about someone laughing on the bus too loudly
  • know who in the group everyone finds irritating
  • register who is censuring who in a meeting find our post censured online by immediate consensus

(and so on)

Humor dress codes are different than humor “guidelines” that we verbally discuss. Asking comedians to not make genocide jokes is a guideline—while the dress code is how an audience would actually respond to specific humor attempt with genocide content.
