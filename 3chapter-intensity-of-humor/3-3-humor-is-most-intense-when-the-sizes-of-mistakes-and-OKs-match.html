Humor is most intense when the sizes of mistakes and OKs balance

To model the intensity of humor, we compare the sizes of mistakes and OKs together. When the sizes of the mistake and OK are balanced, we feel intense humor. 

In other words, we feel humor most intensely when the sizes of the mistake and OK are closest to 1:1—when the two sizes are proportional to each other.

Cheering at games. To think about how balanced sizes lead to intense humor, we can make an analogy to watching scored sports: the closer the scores of both teams, the greater our emotional intensity. 

It doesn’t matter if the scores are both low or both high—as long as they’re both close, we’re on the edge of our seats, laughing, anxious, yelling at the screen, and so on. By contrast, if one team’s score is much larger relative to the other, we’re not always feeling emotions as intensely.

This is similar to how mistakes and OKs interact. When the size of the mistake and OK are close to 1:1, we feel intense humor—whether both large or both small. When they’re not close to 1:1, we feel something other than intense humor.
