Humor outcomes can describe periods of time

We can use humor outcomes to describe individual moments of humor. But we can also use humor outcomes to describe overall experiences of humor:

Xiaowei and Lina find Damien sitting by himself. “Want to come sit with us?” Xiaowei asks. Damien gathers his lunch bag and sits with them.

Xiaowei and Lina are making each other laugh, and take time to explain some inside jokes to Damien. After a while, Damien starts smiling and joins in a bit. (←Lina and Xiaowei help Damien become accepted↔︎humor)

Without knowing anything specifically that was said, we can still describe the overall experience with humor outcomes: throughout the lunch period, Lina and Xiaowei were trying to help Damien become accepted.

And since Damien felt humor, Lina and Xiaowei were successful in getting Damien to feel acceptance, belonging, comfort, connection, welcome, and so on—Damien became accepted.

Scale. Sometimes we think of humor as something that primarily has to do with sentences—and so to discover why someone felt humor, we'd have to have a transcript. But this granular approach can be like thinking we have to look at each tree before we can say there's a forest.

We can use humor outcomes to talk about the collective humor that happened over a general period of time. Lina and Xiaowei helped Damien become accepted—a process that produces humor.

We can say this without knowing any specifics about what they talked about—it's enough to know the gist of what was happening over a period of twenty minutes.
