Chapter 14

Humor Outcomes

In this chapter, we’ll look at humor outcomes. These are ways of describing humor as outcomes:

Mr. Myrtle drops his folder in front of the class, and paper goes everywhere.

Lina and Xiaowei share a glance and smile at each other. (←Lina and Xiaowei created rapport with each other)

Justin and Heather watch a couple and their dog pass.

Justin: (casually) "I don't like dogs."

Heather: *laughs* (←︎Heather feels Justin has revealed a personal view) "What?!"

Jessica: *to a customer* *setting down condiments* “Oh, I love your style!”

Yasmin: *turns red* *brightens* *beams* “Oh. Thanks!” (←Yasmin feels Jessica has symbolized ties)

Trevor is fighting a boss in StormConQuest.

He dies over and over. Finally, after a few hours, he gets within striking distance of victory. He just needs to focus. He can't make a mistake now. He dodges, attacks, dodges, attacks. He leans forward, hands sweating.

Finally, the boss goes down! Trevor laughs and throws himself back on the couch in relief, smiling and feeling completely drained and relaxed. (←Trevor has eased (his own) tension)

Justin is reading a biography about a 10th century philosopher.

The Irish scholar John Eriugena is eating at King Charles' table when John farts.

King Charles: (annoyed) "John, what separates a Scottus [Irishman] from a sottus [a fool]?"

John: *not missing a beat* "Just a table."

Charles: *laughs* (←Charles feels John has handled his criticism)

Justin smiles. John handled that situation perfectly! (←Justin feels John controlled the situation)

Above, moments of humor were moments of eased tension, symbolized ties, revealed personal views, and created rapport.

We'll be talking about humor outcomes the rest of this chapter.
