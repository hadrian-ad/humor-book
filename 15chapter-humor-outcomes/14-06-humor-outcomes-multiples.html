We can use many humor outcomes

We can use one humor outcome to describe the presence or absence of humor—but we can also use many humor outcomes at the same time:

Emma sees Minh down the hall. Uh-oh. She owes him coffee money, and he’s been hurt she hasn’t repaid him yet.

Before he sees her, she dodges down an alternate route. Phew. Safe for another day! She laughs at herself. (←Emma avoids conflict, handles complaints, eases tension, controls a situation)

Sam and Jennifer are taking Riley to the kid-friendly farm. A sign at the goat enclosure says “GOATS HAVE NO MANNERS.”

Sam and Jennifer laugh. (←Sam and Jennifer feel the sign supports an argument, creates rapport, deals with stress, helps create acceptance, handles complaints, acceptably criticizes, symbolizes close ties, controls a situation, avoids conflict, open new topics of conversation)

Riley: (demanding) “What’s so funny?” (←Riley does not feel any humor outcomes)

Overlap. Our humor outcomes all overlap with each other, like how creating rapport ovelaps with easing tension. These humor outcomes all overlap because they’re all re-describing the same thing: humor!

We can use as many or as little humor outcomes as we think is useful. Sometimes, considering several humor outcomes help us see things in new light.
