Introduction

My earliest memory of laughing is very clear—I was six and foolish. I had trapped my arm under a treadmill, and the belt was scraping off skin. I screamed until my dad ran it and turned the treadmill off.

I sobbed as my dad examined my damaged skin, and then—he made me laugh. I remember not wanting to, but the laugh bubbling out anyway.

This was a special talent of my dad—my siblings and I would often share notes about this magic ability of his, to make us laugh when we cried. It seemed inexplicable.

In fact, my dad's odd sense of humor was often a topic of conversation among us—the way he rarely laughed at comedies, but chuckled when he saw people get hurt—or the pleasing way he could summarize books or experiences in a way that would make us laugh.

It was something beyond easy explanation—and so, like most people, we admired the mystery and didn't get much further. A lot of us hover at that point: admiring the mystery without getting further.

This book is designed to give us the nudge to go deeper, to a place where everything clicks into place—the Big Understanding of humor.

I've spent two decades taking notes on smiling and laughing, and developing up a method of teaching and understanding humor—and this book is the end result.

It's my hope that re-encountering humor through this book brings you insight, joy, and wonder.
