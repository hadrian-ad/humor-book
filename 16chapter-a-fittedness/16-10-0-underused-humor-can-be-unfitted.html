The Humor Book

Underused humor can be unfitted

Sometimes, humor is unfitted when it’s overused. (We laugh so hard we miss a customer). But other times, humor is unfitted because it’s underused:

Trevor and Minh are trying to pass each other in the hallway—they do a little dance trying to get around each other.

Minh smiles and Trevor puts his hands on his face in mock frustration, until they finally pass each other. (←humor tells like smiling, touching self, informal movement)

- OR -

They're stone-faced and eventually manage to get by each other. (←no humor tells)

Artem is creating his dating bio.

Artem: *tapping on phone* “Likes GrosTech (early albums), mythology, and games. Did I mention I *really* like GrosTech!?” (←writes with humor tells like informal style, high energy)

- OR -

Artem: *tapping on phone* “Likes GrosTech (early albums), mythology, and games." (←no presented humor tells)

Max is at an event with live music.

He’s loose and smiling, moving a bit to the music, loosely holding a plastic cup. (←humor tells like smiling, informal movement, loosening)

He sees people across the room, and wanders over to see what they’re looking at. He feels like part of the event. (←humor tells like social seeking, social integration)

- OR -

He stands stiffly near the wall holding a plastic cup. He wants to feel involved while somehow also not involving himself. But dancing is stupid, and everything here is dull. From across the room people can see him looking stiff and out of place. (←Max has no humor tells)

In the same way people react negatively to an unfitted excess of humor tells (“ladies!”), people also react negatively to an unfitted absence of humor tells:

Marie: “Jessica! You need to smile more!” (←the manager censures Jessica for her lack of humor tells)

Jessica: *to Emma* “I’ve never seen Marie smile or relax. Not once! It makes me sad.” (←Jessica is frustrated about her manager’s lack of humor tells)

Trevor complains to Yasmin about Minh.

Trevor: (frustrated) “He should lighten up once in a while! He always looks like it’s his last day on earth!” (←Trevor complains about Minh’s lack of humor tells)

Sabrina is going through her dating app and comes across Artem’s bio.

Sabrina: *reading* "'Likes GrosTech (early albums), mythology, and games?' Why does everyone who listens to GrosTech have to take themselves so seriously all the time?" (←Sabrina thinks Artem’s lack of presented humor tells is self-defeating)

Drunk Emma finds Max at the event. He’s standing around like a cadaver with a plastic cup.

Emma: *tipsily* “Max! You hafta relax from time to time, man.” (←Emma censures Max for a lack of humor tells)

In the above examples, it wasn't the presence of humor tells that was unfitted, but their absence.

Intensity. The intensity of humor also has a fittedness. Since humor tells express intensity, the fittedness of intensity is already baked into the fittedness of humor tells. For instance, a loud laugh is fitted in different places than a quiet laugh is.

In these cases, it's not the laugh itself that's unfitted, but the intensity of the laugh that's unfitted.

Other times, it's not too much intensity that's unfitted—it's too little intensity that's unfitted. This is like Jessica's manager, who saw high-intensity humor as unfitted ("ladies!"), along with low-intensity humor ("smile more"). That's because intensity is seen as fitted within a low and high range.
