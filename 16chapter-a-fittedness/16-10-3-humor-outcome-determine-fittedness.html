Fittedness is a measure of efficiency and relevance

The more others see humor or humor attempt as situationally connected, relevant, and multipurpose, the more they see it as fitted:

Carl picks up his sister Sara at the bus station for the funeral. They drive in silence for a while. Finally, Carl says profoundly: “Elias says the world is a place we prove real by dying in it.”

Sara thinks about that.

Then Carl grins: “What an asshole!”

Sara laughs. (←Carl and Sara have achieved catharsis, eased tension, reduced defensiveness, avoided conflict, created rapport, opened up a new topic of conversation, dealt with taboo topics)

Trevor: *shopping online* “Should we order it or not?”

Yasmin: “I just can’t decide! I just love StormConQuest, but the reviews on the expansion are just so mixed. They say StormConQuest: Seas of Drake and Myrrh really simplifies the resource management. What if that ruins it?”

Trevor: *out of the blue* “What do you think about having a kid?” (←Trevor wants to use humor to open a new topic of conversation, reduce defensiveness, reveal a personal view, deal with a taboo topic)

Yasmin: *laughs* “Oh god, Trevor.” (←Yasmin’s laugh shows a new topic has been opened, that she’s dealing with a taboo topic, is easing tensions, revealing a personal view)

Yasmin sees Trevor's humor attempt here as efficiently doing many relevant things at once—and this efficiency is part of why she sees it as fitted.

We understand or tolerate things that are efficient and well-fitted to the situation. This gives them a justification that protects them from pushback.

That's it for this chapter! We'll talk about humor dress codes next.

Emotional actions and intentions have degrees of comparative fit to their emotional situation. We can estimate something's "fittedness" by its integration to a situation.

  • Fittedness focuses on how people do react to humor or humor attempts, not how someone thinks they "should" react.
  • We can estimate fittedness of humor or humor attempts by accounting for relevancy of topics, humor family words, and humor outcomes.
  • Higher fittedness is correlated to greater toleration and increased emotional activity on an emotion network.
