Just like realization and achievement, we feel humor when we feel comfort:

Comfort as an emotion of humor

Zoe takes an early morning jog. She runs through the cold and rain, through the puddles and bleary lights. The weather intensifies the last half mile back to her apartment, and she’s caught in a furious downpour.

When she reaches her apartment, she has to push against the door to close it against the wind. Inside, her apartment is calm and quiet. The rain patters against the windows. “Phew!” Zoe says, laughing. (←comfort↔︎mistake is OK↔︎humor)

As before, we feel humor because of mistakes and OKs. When Jennifer’s at the resort, the furnishing and situation is a mistake—they deviate from what she's come to expect from furnishing and situations. She eventually feels secure from negative personal consequences from that mistake—so she giggles and feels humor. 

The humor we feel from comfort is so familiar that it's often not until it's pointed out that we notice it. But this is like how we smile or laugh when we:

  • enter a public bathroom that turns out to be beautiful
  • put on a hot shirt right out of the dryer
  • finish our exercise routine
  • get into a hot shower after a long day
  • launch our canoe and start fishing
  • look over a prepared feast
  • see a stunning view from a new vantage point
  • taste food that is better than we expected

(and so on)

These are all moments that can make us feel humor. When we want to bundle this humor with its context, we call it comfort.
