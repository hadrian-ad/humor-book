Comfort as humor

Humor family words

Just like realization and achievement, we feel humor when we feel comfort:

As before, we feel humor because mistakes and OKs balance.

When Jennifer’s at the resort, the furnishing and situation deviate from what she's come to expect from furnishing and situations (a mistake). At first she doesn't know how to relate to it, but eventually she leans into the experience (it's OK).

As her mistake and OK balance, she laughs and feels and feels humor. 

Invisible humor. The humor we feel from comfort is so familiar that it's often not until it's pointed out that we notice it—like how we smile or laugh when we:

  • enter a public bathroom that turns out to be beautiful
  • put on a hot shirt right out of the dryer
  • finish our exercise routine
  • get into a hot shower after a long day
  • launch our canoe and start fishing
  • look over a prepared feast
  • see a stunning view from a new vantage point
  • taste food that is better than we expected

(and so on)

These are all moments that can make us feel humor. Whenever we think it would be useful, we can replace the generic word "humor" with a contextual word for humor (comfort).
