The Humor Book

We like artificial scares because we feel safety

One reason we sometimes like being artificially scared is that we feel safety afterwards—we feel humor.

Consider:

This is part of the appeal of something like bungee jumps, haunted houses, jump scares, movie gore, pranks, peek-a-boo, pretend injury, roller coasters, horror films, and so on—we can feel humor from them! 

If we transition from a mistake-predominant emotion into safety, we’ll be feeling humor—if we don’t feel safety, we won’t feel humor:

Trevor and Yasmin go to an Adventure Course and take the zipline down. Yasmin gives a laughing shriek as zips down, and laughs as she’s unharnessed at the bottom. (←Yasmin feels the controlled scare is OK↔︎safety↔︎humor) Trevor goes down the zipline, jaw clenched, face white. (←Trevor feels the controlled scare is not-OK↔︎no safety↔︎no humor)

Trevor is dicing onions. “Careful!” Yasmin says, cringing at how he’s holding the onion.

Trevor smiles and pretends to accidentally cut off a finger, giving a fake cartoonish look of exaggerated pain. Yasmin clutches her face and laughs, “Ahh, stop!” (←Yasmin feels this deception is mostly OK↔︎safety↔︎humor)

- OR -

“Shit!” Trevor drops the knife and puts his finger into his mouth. “I almost severed my pinky!” Yasmin comes over, ashen faced. Then Trevor waves his hand. Just joking. He laughs at her reaction. (←Trevor feels this deception is OK↔︎safety↔︎humor) But Yasmin just stares at him, furious. (←Yasmin feels this deception is not OK↔︎no safety↔︎no humor)

Yasmin gets onto the roller coaster, and it slowly clicks up to the top of the first big drop. Her stomach is twisted as it starts to tip over. (←Yasmin feels the anticipation is not OK↔︎no safety↔︎no humor)

She screams as the car plunges downward! (←Yasmin feels the first drop is not OK↔︎no safety↔︎no humor)

But as they bottom out and start racing along the rest of the track, her face breaks into on open-mouthed smile. (←Yasmin feels fast movement is OK↔︎safety↔︎humor)

Trevor and Yasmin are watching Squeal III.

Rosie explores an old mansion at night, and paused before a small jewelry box ominously on the table. “Don’t do it,” Trevor says, annoyed. Rosie slowly opens the box and a ragged ghost explodes out! 

Trevor and Yasmin leap up. “Fuck!!” Yasmin says, heart thumping. She laughs, hand over her heart. (←Yasmin feels the jump scare is OK ↔︎safety↔︎humor)

Trevor has his hands on his head. “Why can’t they just have the monsters and blood without the freaking jump scares?” he says. (←Trevor feels the jump scare is not OK↔︎no safety↔︎no humor)

Trevor and Yasmin are watching Squeal III.

Rosie screams and throws the switch. The resurrected cultist and former general of the confederacy gets sucked into a wood chipper. As he uses his tentacle hands to grip onto the edges of the feed tray, his bottom half is shredded, intestines flying out the spout, until his entire body is pulled through, coming out as a fine spray of red mist. An eyeball is flung out and splats against a tree like a grape.

“Gro-oss!” Trevor says laughing, shocked. (←Trevor feels the movie gore is OK↔︎safety↔︎humor) Yasmin feels a bit nauseous. (←Yasmin feels the movie gore is not OK↔︎nosafety↔︎no humor)
