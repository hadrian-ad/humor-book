Humor family words

In this chapter, we’ll look at “humor family words.” These are contextual variations of the word humor: 

Humor family words

  • acceptance
  • accomplishment
  • achievement
  • appreciation
  • belonging
  • comfort
  • connection
  • delight
  • forgiveness
  • generosity
  • insight
  • joy
  • realization
  • reconciliation
  • release
  • revelation
  • relief
  • safety
  • success
  • welcome

Humor family words describe moments where we feel humor:

Justin and Heather are mountain biking when Justin flips on a root and tumbles down a small hill. “Oh my god! Justin! Are you okay?” Heather calls, jumping off her bike and scrambling down to the body and bike below.

Justin stirs and sits up. “Oof,” he says, adjusting his helmet shakily. “I’m fine!”

They look at each other and start giggling with relief. (←- mistake is OK↔︎realization, relief, release, safety↔︎humor)

We can describe Heather and Justin’s humor as humor—but also as humor family words, like relief, realization, safety, and so on. We'd use humor family words because they add context.

The word "humor" by itself doesn't imply any context:

When we want to give more context than the word "humor" provides, we use humor family words—like revelation, insight, realization, or so on:

We have more narrative clarity when we use humor family words to talk about humor—since revelation, insight, realization, and so on all evoke more distinctive methods and situations than something like “humor” does by itself.
