The Humor Book

7.1 Realization as humor

Humor family words

acceptance, accomplishment, achievement, appreciation, belonging, comfort, connection, delight, forgiveness, generosity, insight, joy, realization, reconciliation, release, revelation, relief, safety, success, welcome

Since at first it can seem odd to think of humor family words as humor, we'll go through some of our humor family words one-by-one.

We'll see that these words are all humor—all moments where we evaluate mistakes as OK:

Zoe is browsing the bookstore when she picks up a book on meditation. She reads the book title. “Wherever You Go, There You Are.” She thinks about that for a second, puzzled.

Then something clicks. She laughs. (←realization↔︎mistake is OK↔︎humor) 

Zoe laughs after reading a book title: her mistakes and OKs shift in a way that generates humor. In context, we can describe this specific instance of humor as insight, realization, revelation, and so on. These are all different words that describe the same thing: humor.

When mistakes are OK, we often describe this humor in context as realization:

Carol passes a window display.

Carol: (realizing)  “Oh, it was a bong!” *laughs* (←realization↔︎humor)

Jessica: *reading trivia on her bottle of sweet tea* “Emma! Did you know sharks can lose 100 teeth a day!” *laughs* (←realization↔︎humor)

Emma: *not really listening* “Really?”

Zoe: “Ms. Gardener was pretty cool, looking back.”

Emma: “Uh, yeah. Hey, yeah! She was always going to those concerts… EDM concerts, I guess…” (realizing) “…to do drugs!” *laughs* (←realization↔︎humor) “Of course. Duh.”

Jennifer: *texts Emma* “sry for the misunderstanding!! Sam was going to take them to their grandparents but he’s delayed till 3. he was supposed to tell you. Can you watch for 30-45 min? we’ll pay $$”

Emma: *reads text* “Phew!” *laughs with relief* (←realization↔︎humor) *texts Jennifer* “No problem! Enjoy Mexico!”

Alina is talking on the phone with her younger brother, Artem.

Alina: “I’m sorry things are rough right now. Lots of people have troubles after high school. There’s no shame in being depressed. Listen, I have to go, but I’ll call you soon.”

She hangs up. Artem sits back.

Artem: (thunderstruck) “I have depression!?” *laughs* (←realization↔︎humor) “That makes so much sense. Why didn’t I think of that before?”

Yasmin: *getting ready* “I can’t believe I have to blow off Trevor just because Stephen and Tucker wants me to come into work this weekend. Tucker’s an entitled asshole, and Stephen thinks I’m like some kind of slave. God I hate this job. But I have to stick with it, because this is the only way I’ll ever be able to one day have a nice life. I have no choice!”

She stops to think about that, and then laughs. (←realization↔︎humor) “Wow,” she says. “Maybe I should find a new job.” A weight falls off her, and she laughs again. 

We don’t always notice the humor we feel during moments of realization—but once it’s pointed out, we see it everywhere.

Consider:

We used the word realization in the examples above, but we could have used other humor family words too—like insight or revelation.

And while we won’t use these words in this book, we could also use words too, like discovery, reinterpretation, self-discovery, transformation, and so on—all of which describe moments of humor.
