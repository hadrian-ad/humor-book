safety as humor

Humor family words

acceptance, accomplishment, achievement, appreciation, belonging, comfort, connection, delight, forgiveness, generosity, insight, joy, realization, reconciliation, release, revelation, relief, safety, success, welcome

 Another humor family word is safety—when we feel safety, we’re feeling humor: 

Trevor is talking the dog out at night. As General Zod squats to pee, Trevor looks into the nearby patch of dark trees, and a deep fear comes over him. He starts to jog back to the house with the dog until he feels possessed by terror and sprints the final way.

He races inside, locks the door, and breathes a sigh of relief. “Phew. We made it, Zod. Safe!” He laughs as the dog wags its tail. (←safety↔︎mistake is OK↔︎humor)  

Above, Trevor feels humor—he evaluates a mistake as OK. In context, we can describe this humor as safety—which describes humor around close calls, pressing deadlines, high-risk gambles, physical danger, or so on. 

These things sometimes cause us to feel mistake-predominant emotions, like fear. But when these mistakes are eventually matched by an OK, we've moved from fear to safety—we feel humor:

Owen is about to cross the street but Zoe grabs him and pulls him back. A bus careens by, honking. Owen stands there, dumbfounded, knees shaking.

They both look at each other nervously. (←close call is not OK↔︎no safety↔︎no humor)

- OR -

They look at each other, and they both chuckle nervously. (←close call is OK↔︎safety↔︎humor)

Emma has been working overnight to finish her coding project. Setbacks multiply. She pushes on through the morning. The noon deadline creeps closer. This is going to be close. Finally, with two minutes to the deadline, she clicks submit.

This was way too close. She’s exhausted. She shuts her laptop and puts her head over it wearily. (←pressing deadline is not OK↔︎no safety↔︎no humor)

- OR -

When the autograder gives her a B+, she breathes a sigh of relief and puts her feet up. Fuck yeah. She laughs and feels free. (←pressing deadline is OK↔︎safety↔︎humor)

Sam brings his laptop into the bathroom to check on his speculative investments. He doesn’t want Jennifer to know. He doesn’t know what they’ll do if they keep going downward. He holds his breath and checks. 

They’ve lost 85% of their value. He feels sick. He and Jennifer needed that money. (←a high risk gamble is not OK↔︎no safety↔︎no humor)

- OR -

They’ve gone up a good chunk. Phew. He gives a small laugh. Live to fight another day. Maybe things will be okay after all. (←a high risk gamble is OK↔︎safety↔︎humor)

Justin and Heather are mountain biking when Justin flips on a root and tumbles down a small hill. “Oh my god! Justin! Are you okay?” Heather calls, jumping off her bike and scrambling down to the body and bike below.

Justin stirs and tries worriedly to sit up. His legs are tangled in the frame of the bike. “I hope I didn’t break something,” he says. Heather tugs on her helmet with worry. “Oh gosh oh gosh.” (←physical danger is not OK↔︎no safety↔︎no humor)

- OR -

Justin stirs and sits up. “Oof” he says, adjusting his helmet shakily. “I’m fine!” They look at each other and start giggling with relief. (←physical danger is OK↔︎safety↔︎humor)

When Justin gets up, he and Heather giggle with “relief.” Relief is another humor family word. Since relief and safety are context-specific variations of the word humor, both are fine to use simultaneously.

Humor family words overlap because they’re describing the same thing: humor. This is why it usually feels accurate to use more than one humor family word to describe the same emotional experience:

Merging and overlap. When Zoe returns to a warm apartment on a rainy day and smiles, we can describe her emotions as appreciation, belonging, comfort, connection, insight, realization, safety, welcome, and so on. The reason these humor family words overlap with each other is because they're describing the same emotional experience—humor:

We don't have to struggle to pick just one humor family word—since words like appreciation, belonging, comfort, connection, insight, safety, welcome, and so on merge into and redescribe each other. We can use as many of them at once as we feel is useful.
