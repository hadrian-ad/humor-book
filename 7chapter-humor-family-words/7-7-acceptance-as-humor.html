Acceptance as humor

Humor family words

acceptance, accomplishment, achievement, appreciation, belonging, comfort, connection, delight, forgiveness, generosity, insight, joy, realization, reconciliation, release, revelation, relief, safety, success, welcome

 

We’ve seen examples before where we could describe the evaluation of mistakes as OK as acceptance: 

Timothy calls Jennifer.

Timothy: “Jennifer, I know Sam said otherwise, but I checked with the insurance company. They said the treatment is still experimental. They won’t cover it.”

Jennifer is silent for a second. Then she laughs, exhausted.(←acceptance↔︎mistake is OK↔︎humor)

Jennifer: “That’s what I thought. If we hold a fundraiser, I think we should still be able to cover it.”

Sam is examining his nearly bald head in the mirror. He rubs it thoughtfully. Jennifer sees him do this, and she grabs him by the arm. “You look so handsome,” she says, squeezing him. Sam gives a releasing series of small laughs, and leans against her affectionately. (←acceptance↔︎mistake is OK↔︎humor)  

In the examples above, people feel humor when they feel acceptance—since acceptance describes a moment where a mistake is seen as OK. 

Consider:

Sam gives a “releasing series of small laughs”—with release being another of our humor family words. In this case, we can describe his humor as acceptance, connection, comfort, generosity, realization, release, relief, safety, and so on. These words all work, simultaneously, since they’re all describing the same thing—humor.
