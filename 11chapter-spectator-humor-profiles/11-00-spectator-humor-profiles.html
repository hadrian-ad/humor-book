Chapter 11

Spectator Humor Profile

In this chapter, we’ll look at spectator humor profiles—which is how artists, actors, characters, and so on act or depict humor in spectator entertainment.

Consider:

We said before that each person has a humor profile, which is how we express humor tells, specific to ourselves.

But if we act in a play, we won't transplant our personal humor profile to the stage wholesale to act out humor. Instead, we'll use a spectator humor profile.

We can usually tell the difference between the actor pretending humor tells (spectator humor profile) and the actor themselves feeling humor (personal humor profile):

Sam is watching The Trolby Report

Trolby: (in character) "Our enemies say our campaign for city council doesn't have support. Ha! Haha!" (←spectator humor profile) "Well, try saying that to these donors!"

Trolby starts reading off his donor list with intensity. It's just a list of fake names.

The comedian loses his composure and starts laughing. He turns red as he holds paper over his mouth to disguise his laughter. (←personal humor profile)

Sam smiles. (←Sam enjoys when the actor's evaluations break through)

Sam and Jennifer are watching a romantic movie.

The main characters are on sleds at the top of a hill. They smile at each other. (←Spectator humor profile)

They start going down, and the characters' eyes light up. When the woman gets sprayed with powder, she laughs. It's clear the actors are actually having fun. (←personal humor profile)

Sam and Jennifer smile. It's a cute opening. (←personal humor profile)

Sam is watching bloopers for Blood Soul Revenge.

Daisy Girl: "Hah!" (←spectator humor profile) *to usurper* (defiant) “You’ll never find Rage Man. He’s a bladed shadow. He’s crimson moonlight. He treads the mmhmm…” *forgets line*

The actor playing Daisy Girl throws up her hands and laughs. (←personal humor profiles)

Sam smiles. (←personal humor profile)

Sam asks a stranger to take a picture of him, Jennifer, and Riley.

Stranger: "Okay, one, two, three, smile!"

Sam, Jennifer, and Riley all give stilted camera smiles as the stranger takes some pictures. (←spectator humor profiles)

Stranger: "Okay, got them!"

Sam and Jennifer relax and they start laughing and smiling at each other. (←personal humor profiles)

Unbeknownst to them, the stranger keeps taking photos, creating two sets—one of them awkwardly smiling at the camera, and another of them smiling at each other.

Sam doesn't have trouble sorting through what's a humor profile, and what's a spectator humor profile.

We'll be talking more about spectator humor profiles throughout this chapter.
