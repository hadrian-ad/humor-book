Chapter 11

Spectator Humor Profile

Last chapter we looked at spectator entertainment. In this chapter, we’ll look at how artists, actors, characters, and so on depict humor in spectator entertainment—spectator humor profiles.

Consider:

We said before that each person has a humor profile. Our humor profile is how we express humor tells, specific to ourselves.

But if we act in a play, we won't transplant our actual humor profile to the stage wholesale. Instead, we'll use a spectator humor profile.

We can usually tell the difference between the actor acting humor tells (spectator humor profile) and the actor themselves feeling humor (personal humor profile)—in part because the humor we feel is so much more nuanced and vivid than the humor we can act out:

We don’t always notice how artificial acted humor is until someone points it out—but then it's something that's hard to unsee.

We'll be talking more about spectator humor profiles throughout this chapter.
