The Humor Book

Animation can make many noises seem like laughter

While live-action spectator entertainment doesn't often have memorable laughs, animated spectator entertainment often does.

For instance, most of the characters in The Simpsons have distinctive laughs. If we’re familiar with the show, we can propably recall how most of the cast laughs:

  • Marge has a nasal “Hm!hmhmhm”
  • Homer has a hearty “BWAH!ha-ha-ha-ha!”
  • Bart has a troublemaker’s “eh heh heh hah ha”
  • Lisa has a snacking “hheh hheeh haw haw.”

(and so on)

By contrast, our favorite live action shows probably has less laughing overall, and fewer distinctive laughs.

Consider:

Even though we interpret characters in animated spectator entertainment as laughing, the actors are more often than not just making noises. Many repetitive noises will be interpreted as laughter when superimposed over cartoon animations of laughter.

This is why we can remember so many distinctive laugh-like noises for animated characters—voice actors have practical leeway to create distinctive vocal characetrizations for their characters. All without worrying about how they look while making these laugh-like noises!

We relate to characters with coherent, vivid spectator humor profiles. This is one reason we often find characters in animated comedies to be emotionally responsive and relatable—while sitcom characters often feel flat by comparison.

Memorability. Some live-action characters have memorable humor tells, like:

  • Han Solo’s lopsided smile
  • Fox Mulder’s enthusiastic grin
  • James Bond’s smirk
  • Harry Potter’s shy smile
  • Jack Sparrow's half-of-face smile

(and so on)

But even though these humor tells are memorable, they probably don't have memorable laughs—because it's hard to create a signature laugh for a live-action character, let alone do it convincingly on command.

We can find scenes of them laughing, but the sound of the laugh itself is often forgettable—a video version of a novel saying "he laughed."

We can compare this to Homer Simpson—who has an iconic laugh that fans would instantly recognize.

Consider:

This disparity in laughter is one reason live-action characters don't always translate well to animation and vice versa—a live actor for Homer would have to cut the laugh, while an animated Han Solo wouldn't have a laugh!
