Distinquishing represented humor

When put side-by-side, it's often easy to distinquish between an actor acting humor (spectator humor profile) and the actors own feelings of humor (personal humor profile):

Pretended evaluations are less vividly presented than our actual evaluations—which isn't unique to humor, but is true for all our emotions.

Consider:

We can usually distinquish between when someone is pretending to be surprised and when they are actually surprised.

Pretended humor tends to seem flat everywhere—whether in social situations or in movies!

Limitations. Spectator humor profiles are partially the result of physical limitations—we simply can't convincingly act medium-intensity or higher humor on command!

We can't convincingly do our own laugh on command—let alone imitate someone else's laughter. We can sometimes caricature, but not replicate intense laughter.

Live action movies avoid asking their actors to do things they are incapable of doing—which leads to movies that are devoid of in-movie laughter. While we don't always notice this about movies, once it's pointed out it's hard to unsee!
