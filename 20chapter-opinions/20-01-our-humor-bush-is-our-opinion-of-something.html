The Humor Book

Our humor bush is our opinion of something

If Emma says she has a high opinion of Seattle, her good opinion is based on her humor bush with Seattle:

Emma: “I went to Seattle last summer! I love Seattle!” (←Emma’s opinion of Seattle)

Minh: “I lived there for eighteen years. I… like it.” (←Minh’s opinion of Seattle)

Owen: “Well I hate Seattle.” (←Owen’s opinion of Seattle) “I was trapped in a Greyhound station there for like twelve hours overnight once. One of the worst experiences of my life!”

When Emma says she loves Seattle, and Owen says he hates it, they’re not arguing about the merits of Seattle’s:

  • strong mayoral system
  • growth opportunities in their retail market
  • economic data of the middle quintile of residents

(or so on)

Instead, Emma loves how she feels about Seattle—just like Owen hates how he feels about Seattle. They’re both describing their humor bush with Seattle.

Consider:

When Emma visited Seattle, she felt she frequently created rapport (with herself or the city), frequently gained new perspectives, constantly avoided conflict (with herself or others while travelling), felt easily able to become accepted, constantly symbolized close ties (with herself, the city, the world), and so on.

By contrast, Owen felt he never created rapport (with himself or the city), rarely gained new perspectives, couldn’t avoid conflict, never symbolized close ties, and so on.

Emma visited Seattle for a vacation—it was a short time period that was filled with interesting and novel experiences. This gave her a large humor bush.

By contrast, Minh lived there—it was a long time period that was filled with less straightforwardly enjoyable things. This gives Minh a smaller overall humor bush than Emma:

Since Minh’s humor bush with Seattle is smaller than Emma, his opinion of the city is smaller than Emma's opinion—but not as low as Owen’s opinion.

Dilution. Emma has one topic 'Seattle,' which she attributes all humor to—this supercharges her humor bush with 'Seattle.'

By contrast, Minh has many active topics he uses instead of Seattle—he attributes humor to his neighborhood, his home, his school, a specific shop, and so on. This dilutes his humor across many topics, instead of all being attributed to 'Seattle.'

Minh may have a large humor bush with his home street in Seattle—and his face may light up when it's mentioned. But unlike Emma, he won't neccessarily attribute this localized humor with the abstract city 'Seattle' itself.
