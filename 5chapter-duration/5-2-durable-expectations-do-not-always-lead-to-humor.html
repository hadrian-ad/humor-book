A long mistake doesn't mean long humor

The duration of a mistake isn't the same thing as the duration of humor. That's because humor only happens when the mistakes and OKs balance.

So a long mistake won't generate long humor unless it's also balanced by an OK:

The duration of humor is controlled by how long a mistake and OK are balanced: 

Minh and Trevor check their co-op score. 469.

Trevor: “69. Nice.”

Minh: "Heh" (←Trevor's joke doesn't go against Minh's durable expectations of Trevor's behavior↔︎relatively shorter duration of humor)

- OR -

Minh and his mom check their co-op score. 469.

Mom: "69. Nice."

Minh: (surprised) "Mom!" *laughs* (←Minh's mom's joke goes against Minh's durable expectations of his mother's behavior↔︎longer duration humor)  

Justin: *telegraphing joke* *raises eyebrow* (amused) “… And so then the manager says: two rights don’t make a wrong!”

Heather: *smiles* “Har har.” (←Heather quickly acclimates↔︎shorter humor)

- OR -

Justin: *talking normally* “… and, anyway, then I told Dave right up front that he shouldn’t do that—two rights don’t make a wrong!”

Heather: *laughs* “You mean two wrongs don’t make a right!”

Justin: (perplexed) “That’s what I…” *catches self* “Oh. Oh!” *laughs*

Heather: *laughs* (←Heather slowly acclimates↔︎longer humor)  

When Justin telegraphs the joke, Heather quickly acclimates to the mistake. Since she soon has a medium mistake and a large OK, she feels a short duration of humor.

By contrast, Justin rarely makes speaking errors—which builds up durable expectations for Heather. Since her medium mistake and medium OK balance for a period of time, she has a relatively longer duration of humor.
