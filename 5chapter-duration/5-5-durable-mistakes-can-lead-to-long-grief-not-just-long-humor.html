Long mistakes don't always lead to long humor

Something that deviates from durable expectations can lead to long periods of humor—but it can also lead to long periods of mistake-predominant emotions.

If our OK is lower than our durable mistake, we'll feel long duration of grief or sadness:

The wooded lot near their home was cleared. It causes Jennifer grief that year. (←Jennifer feels long duration of grief) 

Similarly, mistakes can recur in a way that doesn't continually amuse us, but that continually unsettles us instead:

Jennifer and Sam had to downsize houses last year. Sometimes she has to drive past her old house, and each time she feels a sharp loss. (←Jennifer feels long duration of grief)

We experience long durations of mistake-predominant emotions all the time, like when: 

  • a friend dies
  • a limb is lost
  • a partner leaves us
  • a nearby forest is developed
  • we do something we think is shameful
  • we become displaced

(and so on)

The same process that prolongs our humor is what prolongs our anger or sadness as well.
