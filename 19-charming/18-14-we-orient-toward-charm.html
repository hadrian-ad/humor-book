Charm makes up our emotional orientation

We can think of humor family words as the emotional orientation of our lives. We emotionally orient toward acceptance, achievement, appreciation, comfort, connection, delight, generosity, insight, joy, revelation, relief, safety, welcome, and so on.

All things being equal, we emotionally orient ourselves around humor family words. Which means we orient toward the people, moments, memory, things, and so on that we find charming—that help us feel humor family words.

All things being equal, we position ourselves to feel a humor family words:

There are two coffee shops on the street. One makes her uncomfortable, the other makes her feel welcome. She goes into the one that makes her feel welcome.

When Zoe was young, teachers praised her for reading so much. She still enjoys reading, and associates it with achievement. As she got older, she reads to learn about new things and other experiences in the world. It relaxes her.

Minh has the option of going to a game night for the first time, or hanging out with Amaya. He chooses to hang out with Amaya.

Trevor listens to podcasts on his long drives back home. He stops a boring one, and tries another one. It's so interesting! He devours it.

Heather used to live beneath a couple who would frequently get into serious domestic arguments. It made her feel unsafe, and looks back on that time with a sick feeling.
