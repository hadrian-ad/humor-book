The Humor Book

We have to allow ourselves to feel fitted humor as well

To be charming, it’s not enough to make others feel fitted humor—we have to allow ourselves to feel fitted humor as well.

Zoe’s goal isn’t to make Sara feel humor while herself emotionally guarding against humor—instead, Zoe has to also express humor when Sara tries to be charming in return.

Sara sets her backpack down and admires Zoe’s plants.

Sara: “You’ve always done such a good job with your plants. I can barely keep that succulent you gave me alive, let alone look after twenty pots!”

Zoe: *smiling* (←Zoe allows herself to feel achievement↔︎Zoe accepts charm↔︎humor) (deflecting) “Well. Thanks! It’s not that big a deal after a while, they’re just plants.”

- OR -

Zoe: *embarrassed* (←Zoe doesn’t allow herself to feel achievement↔︎Zoe declines charm↔︎no humor) (deflecting) “Oh, it’s not a big deal, they’re just plants.”

Over time, mutual fitted humor allows for mutual feelings of appreciation, connection, reconciliation, welcome, and so on—which accomplishes Zoe’s goal of mutually renewing their friendship.

This is why Zoe defaults giveing greater priority to fittedness than intensity. Fittedness is more in-line than her long-term objectives than intensity is by itself.

Default. Sometimes we default to comedy attempts, like if we've fallen into class clown roles, or develop a habit of undercutting moments of connection or delight. When we default this way, we sometimes run into awkward moments or hurt others, and don't develop close connections with others.

That's why we default to charm attempts, since fitted humor is what allows us to have functioning and enjoyable experiences with each other. It allows us to mutually feel acceptance, belonging, connection, generosity, welcome, and so on.

And by defaulting to charm, we improve our comedy attempts too—since defaulting to charm give ourselves the space to pick and choose when we make comedy attempts. This gives us the best of both worlds: the ability to consistently make fitted humor attempts, interspersed with moments of high-intensity humor.
