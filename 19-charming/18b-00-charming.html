Chapter 23

Charm

Last chapter, we looked at charm—judging or prioritizing humor or humor attempts by fittedness, not intensity.

In this chapter, we'll look at being charming—which is many instances of charm.

Minh watches Amaya strike up a conversation with a lady on the bus. Amaya has her laughing and they start chatting.

Minh scratches his head. How does Amaya do that? It’s something he really admires about her. (←charm is involved in easily making friends↔︎creating rapport, easing tension, symbolizing ties, revealing personal views, opening new topics of conversation, and so on)

- LATER -

Minh’s soup is cold. Amaya calls over the server.

Minh: (nervous) “It’s fine. Let’s just leave it.”

The server comes over and Amaya smiles and explains that the soup is cold. Amaya and the server both end up smiling and amused, and she takes the plate away to bring back warm soup. (←charm is involved in criticizing things without angering people↔︎acceptably criticizing, easing tension, opening new topics of conversation, giving new perspectives, supporting an argument, and so on)

- LATER -

Minh is worrying about how his sculpture will be critiqued, and he mentions it to Amaya. What if the panel hates it?

Amaya: “Minh, I’ve seen it! You want my genuine opinion? It’s a totally wonderful piece of art.”

Minh: “You really think so?"

Amaya: “You think so too!”

Minh: “Yeah. Yeah I guess I do!” *laughs* (←charm is involved in Amaya projecting optimism and changing views↔︎insight, realization, release, relief, safety, and so on)

Since Amaya regularly generates fitted humor in Minh and others, Minh finds her to be charming.

Consider:

If we’re judging by intensity, then Amaya's humor attempts would be bad to okay instances of comedy. But if we're judging by fittedness, these humor attempts are okay to good instances of charm.

We'll be taking this broader look at fitted humor (being charming) throughout this chapter.

Description. Last chapter, we looked at how we can feel fitted humor on a walk. We'd describe our humor tells as appreciation, belonging, comfort, delight, forgiveness, release, safety, welcome, and so on:

But we wouldn't feel all these things briefly once and then never again the rest of the walk. Instead, we'd probably feel fitted humor continuously at various things over a few hours—we'd continually experience or notice new things (mistakes) and feel fine about them (OK).

In that sense, we can say it's a charming walk—there's continuous or frequent instances of charm. Minh feels something similar about Amaya, for similar reasons—he finds her charming, due to continous or frequent instances of fitted humor.
