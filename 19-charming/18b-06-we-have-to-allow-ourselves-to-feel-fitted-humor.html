Charmingness is often a two-way street

To be charming, it’s not enough to make others feel fitted humor—we have to allow ourselves to feel fitted humor too.

Zoe’s goal isn’t to make Sara feel humor while herself emotionally guarding against humor—instead, Zoe has to also express humor when Sara tries to be charming in return.

Sara sets her backpack down and admires Zoe’s plants.

Sara: “You’ve always done such a good job with your plants. I can barely keep that succulent you gave me alive, let alone look after twenty pots!”

Zoe: *embarrassed* (←Zoe doesn’t allow herself to feel achievement↔︎Zoe declines charm↔︎no humor) (deflecting) “Oh, it’s not a big deal, they’re just plants.”

- OR -

Zoe: *smiling* (←Zoe allows herself to feel achievement↔︎Zoe accepts charm↔︎humor) (deflecting) “Well. Thanks! It’s not that big a deal after a while, they’re just plants.”

Over time, mutual fitted humor allows for mutual feelings of appreciation, connection, reconciliation, welcome, and so on—which accomplishes Zoe’s goal of mutually renewing their friendship.

This is why Zoe defaults to prioritizing fittedness instead of intensity. Fittedness is more in-line than her long-term objectives than intensity is by itself.

Default. Sometimes we default to comedy attempts, like if we fall into jester or class clown roles, or develop a habit of undercutting moments of connection or delight. When this is our default, we sometimes run into awkward moments, hurt others, or don't develop close connections with others.

That's why we usually have the best outcomes when we default to charm attempts, since fitted humor is what allows us to have functioning and enjoyable experiences with each other. It allows us to develop reflexes that go toward generating mutually felt acceptance, belonging, connection, generosity, welcome, and so on.

Defaulting to charm doesn't mean we give up comedy attempts—it just means we don't reflexively make them. This gives us the space to pick and choose when we make comedy attempts, minimizing the bad outcomes or wasted opportunities that can happen when comedy attempts are made thoughtlessly.
