Humor outcomes add context to the word humor

We can use humor outcomes to describe individual moments of humor. But we can also use humor outcomes to describe the humor overall in a situation:

Xiaowei and Lina find Damien sitting by himself. “Want to come sit with us?” Xiaowei asks. Damien gathers his lunch bag and sits with them.

Xiaowei and Lina are making each other laugh, and take time to explain some inside jokes to Damien. After a while, Damien starts smiling and joins in a bit. (←Lina and Xiaowei help Damien become accepted↔︎humor)

We can use humor outcomes to describe Damien’s humor over a longer timescale: throughout the lunch period, Lina and Xiaowei were trying to help Damien become accepted.

Since Damien was feeling humor, they were successful—Damien felt acceptance, belonging, comfort, connection, welcome, and so on.

Consider:

Humor outcomes make it easier to talk about collective humor that happens over longer periods of time. We don't need a transcript to talk about the whys and hows of Damien's humor. Instead, we can just say Lina and Xiaowei helping Damien become accepted.

Sometimes we think we need to dig through conversations sentence-by-sentence to discover what generates humor. Sometimes this can make us miss the forest for the trees—and simply thinking about humor outcomes on a longer timescale can help understand broader changes happening in the emotional situation.
