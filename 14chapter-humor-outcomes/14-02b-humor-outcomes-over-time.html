Humor outcomes over time

We used humor outcomes to describe individual moments of humor. But we can also use humor outcomes to describe the humor in a situation overall:

Xiaowei and Lina find Damien sitting by himself. “Want to come sit with us?” Xiaowei asks. Damien gathers his lunch bag and sits with them.

Xiaowei and Lina are making each other laugh, and take time to explain some inside jokes to Damien. After a while, Damien starts smiling and joins in a bit. (←Lina and Xiaowei help Damien become accepted↔︎humor)

Damien felt humor over a period of twenty minutes. We can describe this longer timescale of humor with humor outcomes: throughout the lunch period, Lina and Xiaowei were trying to help Damien become accepted.

Because Damien felt humor, Line and Xiaowei were successful in getting Damien to feel acceptance, belonging, comfort, connection, welcome, and so on—he became accepted.

Consider:

We don't always need to dig through conversations sentence-by-sentence to discover what generates humor—since this moment-by-moment approach can make us miss the forest for the trees.

We don't need a transcript before we can talk about Damien's humor. Instead, we can use humor outcomes to talk about the collective humor that happens over periods of time: taken together, Lina and Xiaowei helped Damien become accepted.
