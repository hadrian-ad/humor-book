The Humor Book

Humor outcomes have intensity

Humor outcomes have intensity:

Zoe is browsing the bookstore when she picks up a book on meditation. She reads the book title: Wherever You Go, There You Are.

She thinks about that for a second, puzzled. Then something clicks.

She gives an amused nose-exhale. (←low-intensity humor↔︎low-intensity of gain a new perspective)

- OR -

She laughs aloud, tickled. She smiles after she puts the book back for a while. (←high-intensity humor↔︎high-intensity of gain a new perspective)

Lina and Xiaowei try to include Damien in their conversations. He mostly gives weak smiles and still looks pretty tense, even by the end of the period. (←low-intensity humor↔︎low-intensity of becoming accepted)

- OR -

Lina and Xiaowei try to include Damien in their conversations. Soon, he’s laughing and leaning over his lunch tray comfortably. (←high-intensity humor↔︎high-intensity of becoming accepted)

Emma sees Minh down the hall. Uh-oh. She owes him coffee money, and he’s been hurt she hasn’t repaid him yet.

Before he sees her, she dodges down an alternate route. Phew. Safe for another day! She laughs at herself. (←low-intensity humor↔︎low-intensity avoids conflict)

- OR -

Emma walks up to Minh.

Emma: “So, erm, I think I owe you some money.”

Minh: (surprised) “Really? For what?”

Emma: “Coffee?”

Minh: (confused) “What? I was just paying you back for when you let me use your meal plan.”

Emma: “Oh. Oh yeah! Right.”

Minh leaves. Emma laughs with relief. (←high-intensity humor↔︎high-intensity avoids conflict)

Guess she hadn’t needed to avoid him after all! She moves lightly down the hall.

When Emma dodges down the hall, she feels she avoided conflict. But since her humor wasn’t particularly long-lasting or intense, Emma didn’t intensely feel she avoided conflict.

By contrast, if Emma realizes there had never been any potential conflict—so that she feels she completely avoided conflict—she feels more intense humor for longer.

Modification. We can always change the language of a humor outcome to reflect a change in intensity:

  • At low-intensity humor, someone may feel they just somewhat influence a situation
  • At high-intensity humor, someone may feel they completely control a situation
  • At low-intensity humor, someone may just feel they temporarily push back conflict
  • At high-intensity humor, someone may feel they completely avoid conflict.

To keep things readable in this book, we'll just use static phrases for humor outcomes. But we can often be more accurate by using either milder or more intense phrasing on a case-by-case basis.
