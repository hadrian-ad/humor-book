The Humor Book

Humor outcomes have intensity

The more intense the humor, the more intense the humor outcome:

Lina and Xiaowei try to include Damien in their conversations. He mostly gives weak smiles and still looks pretty tense, even by the end of the period. (←low-intensity humor↔︎low-intensity of becoming accepted)

- OR -

Lina and Xiaowei try to include Damien in their conversations. Soon, he’s laughing and leaning over his lunch tray comfortably. (←high-intensity humor↔︎high-intensity of becoming accepted)

Zoe is browsing the bookstore when she picks up a book on meditation. She reads the book title: “Wherever You Go, There You Are.”

She thinks about that for a second, puzzled. Then something clicks, and she gives an amused nose-exhale. (←low-intensity humor↔︎low-intensity of gain a new perspective)

- OR -

Zoe is browsing the bookstore when she picks up a book on meditation. She reads the book title: “Wherever You Go, There You Are.”

She thinks about that for a second, puzzled. Then something clicks, and she laughs aloud, tickled. She smiles after she puts the book back for a while. (←high-intensity humor↔︎high-intensity of gain a new perspective)

Emma sees Minh down the hall. Uh-oh. She owes him coffee money, and he’s been hurt she hasn’t repaid her yet. Before he sees her, she dodges down an alternate route. Phew.

Safe for another day! She laughs at herself. (←low-intensity humor↔︎low-intensity avoids conflict)

- OR -

Emma: “So, erm, I think I owe you some money.”

Minh: (surprised) “Really? For what?”

Emma: “Coffee?”

Minh: (confused) “What? I was just paying you back for when you let me use your meal plan.”

Emma: “Oh. Oh yeah! Right.”

Minh leaves. Emma laughs with relief. (←high-intensity humor↔︎high-intensity avoids conflict)

Guess she hadn’t needed to avoid him after all! She moves lightly down the hall.

When Emma dodges down the hall, she reaches a few different humor outcomes—she avoided conflict (by postponing it), handled complaints (by avoiding them), eased (her own) tension, controlled a situation, and so on. But since her humor wasn’t particularly long-lasting or intense, Emma didn’t intensely feel she avoided conflict or controlled a situation.

By contrast, when Emma realizes there had never been any potential conflict—that she completely avoided conflict and completely eased (her own) tension—she feels a longer duration of high-intensity humor.

Consider:

We can change the language of a humor outcome to reflect its intensity. At low-intensity humor, someone may just somewhat influence a situation. But at very intense humor, someone may completely control a situation.

Similarly, at less intense humor, we we may just reduce conflict—while at intense humor we may say we've completely avoided conflict.

Overlap. Our humor outcomes all overlap with each other—like how creating rapport is similar to easing tension. These humor outcomes all overlap because they’re all re-describing the same thing: humor!

In a specific situation, some humor outcomes will feel like precise descriptions, while the rest will feel like imprecise descriptions. We usually stick with the precise descriptions for intuitive clarity.

At the same time, all humor outcomes describe all moments of humor in a loose, poetic sense—like how creating rapport is interestingly similar to something like supporting an argument—or how supporting an argument is interestingly similar to gain a new perspective—or how gain a new perspective is interestingly similar to ease tension—and so on.

These phrases interlock and overlap because they’re different ways of describing the same thing: humor.
