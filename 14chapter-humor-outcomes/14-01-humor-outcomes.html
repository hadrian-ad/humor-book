The Humor Book

There are many outcomes of humor

We'll use a specific list of humor outcomes in this book:

  • Support an argument
  • Change negative emotions into positive ones
  • Create rapport
  • Ease tension
  • Give new perspectives
  • Help one become accepted
  • Reduce defensiveness
  • Reveal a personality trait
  • Reveal a personal view
  • Survive in high stress situations
  • Handle complaints
  • Deal with taboo topics
  • Open up a new topic of conversation
  • Acceptably criticize
  • Symbolize close ties
  • Control a situation
  • Achieve catharsis
  • Avoid conflict

These humor outcomes are the culmination of actions. This is why each humor outcome starts with a verb: we help someone become accepted, ease tension, support an argument, survive in high stress situations, and so on.

Since it’s easier to think about actions than it is to think about invented terminology (“mistakes and OKs”), we can use humor outcomes to think quickly and effectively about humor.

Actions and states. Humor outcomes and humor family words are similar in that both are situation-referential replacements for the generic word “humor.”

But humor family words and humor outcomes are different in that humor family words are states (achievement, insight, reconciliation, safety, and so on) while humor outcomes are actions (control a situation, avoid conflict, and so on).

Together, humor outcomes and humor family words provide two different ways of thinking about the same thing: humor.
