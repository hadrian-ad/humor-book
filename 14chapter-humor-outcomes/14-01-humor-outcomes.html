Humor outcomes describe arrangements of mistakes and OKs

Humor outcomes

acceptably criticize, achieve catharsis, avoid conflict, change negative emotions into positive ones, control a situation, create rapport, deal with taboo topics, ease tensions, gain new perspectives, handle complaints, help one become accepted, open up a new topic of conversation, reduce defensiveness, reveal a personality trait, reveal a personal view, support an argument, survive in high stress situations, symbolize close ties.

A humor outcome is the same thing as humor:

Zoe is browsing the bookstore when she picks up a book on meditation. She reads the book title: Wherever You Go, There You Are.

She thinks about that for a second, puzzled. Then something clicks, and she gives an amused nose-exhale. (←humor↔︎a mistake is OK↔︎Zoe gained a new perspective)

Above, we're describing the same thing in three interchangeable ways:

  • Zoe feels humor
  • Zoe's evaluates a mistake and OK as balancing
  • Zoe gains a new perspective (←humor described as its outcome)

We could use any of the descriptions above to talk about Zoe's humor. We pick between them based on what's helpful.

consider:

We can use humor outcomes to talk about humor in narrative and tangible ways.

We don't convey any situational context when we say Zoe "felt humor" or that she "evaluated a mistake as balanced with an OK." We can include context by instead saying she gained a new perspective.

Sameness. At first, it can seem odd to say humor outcomes like gain a new perspetive is humor. But we've already seen how this is humor in our chapter on humor family words.

In that chapter, we looked at how words like insight, realization, revelation, and so on are humor. These words are very similar to phrases like gain a new perspective.

In other words, when Zoe gains a new perspetive, she's feeling insight, realization, revelation, and so on—she's feeling humor.
