The Humor Book

Humor outcomes describe arrangements of mistakes and OKs

Humor outcomes

acceptably criticize, achieve catharsis, avoid conflict, change negative emotions into positive ones, control a situation, create rapport, deal with taboo topics, ease tensions, give new perspectives, handle complaints, help one become accepted, open up a new topic of conversation, reduce defensiveness, reveal a personality trait, reveal a personal view, support an argument, survive in high stress situations, symbolize close ties.

To start making more sense of humor outcomes, we can look at one of them—give new perspectives.

Gain a new perspective is a humor outcome because it references moments in a situation where our mistakes and OKs balance:

Zoe is browsing the bookstore when she picks up a book on meditation. She reads the book title: Wherever You Go, There You Are.

She thinks about that for a second, puzzled. Then something clicks, and she gives an amused nose-exhale. (←humor↔︎a mistake is OK↔︎Zoe gained a new perspective)

Above, we're describing the same thing in three interchangeable ways:

  • Zoe feels humor
  • Zoe's evaluation of a mistake and OK balance
  • Zoe gains a new perspective (←humor described as its outcome)

We could use any of the descriptions above to talk about Zoe's humor.

consider:

Humor outcomes have a specific benefit: they bundle context to humor, giving a narratively intuitive way of talking and thinking about humor.

It's situationally vague to say that Zoe "felt humor" or that she "evaluated a mistake as balanced with an OK." This indescrimately describes millions of experiences in her life! By saying instead that she gained a new perspective we provide a framing to her evaluatory activity.

Sameness. In this book, gain a new perspective is the same thing as humor. At first this can seem odd, especially for a humor outcome like gain a new perspective—but we've already seen how they are the same.

In our chapter on humor family words, we saw how words like insight, realization, revelation, and so on are humor. When we say Zoe gained a new perspective, we're talking about her feeling insight, realization, revelation, and so on.

For the same reason a humor family word like realization is humor, gaining a new perspective is humor.
