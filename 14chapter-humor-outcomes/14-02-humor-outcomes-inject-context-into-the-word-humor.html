The Humor Book

Humor outcomes add context to the word humor

Humor outcomes

acceptably criticize, achieve catharsis, avoid conflict, change negative emotions into positive ones, control a situation, create rapport, deal with taboo topics, ease tensions, give new perspectives, handle complaints, help one become accepted, open up a new topic of conversation, reduce defensiveness, reveal a personality trait, reveal a personal view, support an argument, survive in high stress situations, symbolize close ties.

Humor outcomes are interchangeable with the emotion of humor. That’s because humor outcomes are like a descriptive skin we can put over "balanced mistakes and OKs":

As Owen scrolls through his camera roll, he sees several similar photos on his phone. He looks at them for a while, and finally he closes out and laughs regretfully. “What a creeper. Ah, fuck me.” He feels lighter.

Then he laughs again, feeling better. (←mistake and OK are balanced↔︎Owen achieved catharsis↔︎humor)

Zoe is browsing the bookstore when she picks up a book on meditation. She reads the book title: “Wherever You Go, There You Are.”

She thinks about that for a second, puzzled. Then something clicks, and she gives an amused nose-exhale. A funny sort of realization. (←mistake and OK are balanced↔︎Zoe gained a new perspective↔︎humor)

Trevor: *shopping online* “Should we order it or not?”

Yasmin: “I just can’t decide! I just love StormConQuest: The Board Game, but the reviews on the expansion are just so mixed. They say StormConQuest: Seas of Drake and Myrrh really overcomplicates the resource management. What if that ruins it?”

Trevor: *out of the blue* “What do you think about having a kid?” (←Trevor wants to open a new topic of conversation)

Yasmin: *puts hand on face* *laughs* “Oh god, Trevor. That’s not StormConQuest!” (←Yasmin feels mistake and OK are balanced↔︎Trevor opened a new topic of conversation↔︎humor)

Trevor: *laughs* “Not now obviously. Just spitballing here, random talk.” (←Trevor feels mistake and OK are balanced↔︎Trevor feels he opened a new topic of conversation↔︎humor)

Zoe comes in from the rain, and has to push against the door to close it against the wind. Inside, her apartment is calm and quiet, and the rain is spattering against the windows. “Phew!” Zoe says, laughing.(←mistake and OK are balanced↔︎Zoe has changed negative emotions into positive ones↔︎humor)

Sam is examining himself in the mirror, looking at his balding head. He rubs it thoughtfully. Jennifer sees him do this, and she grabs him by the arm.

Jennifer: *squeezing him* “You look so handsome,” (←Jennifer wants to help Sam evaluate a mistake as OK↔︎Jennifer wants to ease tension, help Sam feel accepted, change Sam’s negative emotions into positive ones)

Sam gives a releasing series of small laughs and leans against her affectionately. (←Sam’s mistake and OK are balanced↔︎Sam’s tension has eased, Sam feels accepted, Sam’s negative emotions have changed into positive ones↔︎humor)

When humor is absent, there’s been no humor outcome:

As Owen scrolls through his camera roll, he sees several similar photos on his phone. He looks at them for a while, and finally he closes the app. “Ah, fuck me,” he says. He feels miserable. (←mistake and OK are not balanced↔︎Owen has not achieved catharsis↔︎no humor)

Zoe comes in from the rain and has to push against the door to close it against the wind. Inside, her apartment is small and cold, the harsh light of the nearby pharmacy shining into her window through the rain.

“Oof,” Zoe says, dispirited. (←mistake and OK are not balanced↔︎Zoe has not changed negative emotions into positive ones↔︎no humor)

Trevor: *out of the blue* “What do you think about having a kid?” (←Trevor wants to open a new topic of conversation)

Yasmin: *sighs* “I don’t really think this is a good time to talk about this.” (←mistake and OK are not balanced↔︎Yasmin feels that Trever has not opened new topic of conversation↔︎no humor)

Trevor: (pressing on) *lump in his throat* “But we don’t have to decide anything, I just thought we should, you know, spitball.” (←Trevor continues to try to open a new topic of conversation, but feels he has not done so↔︎no humor)

Yasmin: “Trevor. I don’t want to talk about this now.” (←Yasmin feels that Trevor has not opened a new topic of conversation↔︎no humor)

Sometimes the generic word "humor" doesn't give much clarity to what's happening in an emotional situation. By using humor outcomes, we can better describe what's happening when humor happens.
