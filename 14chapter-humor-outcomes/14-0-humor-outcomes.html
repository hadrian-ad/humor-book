14. Humor Outcomes

In this chapter, we’ll look at humor outcomes. These are ways of re-describing "balancing mistakes and OKs" as actions or outcomes.

For instance, in some situations, balancing our evaluations of mistakes and OKs is the same thing as gaining a new perspective. This means gain a new perspective is a humor outcome:

Zoe is browsing the bookstore when she picks up a book on meditation. She reads the book title: “Wherever You Go, There You Are.”

She thinks about that for a second, puzzled. Then something clicks, and she gives an amused nose-exhale. (←humor↔︎a mistake is OK↔︎Zoe gained a new perspective)

Above, we're describing the same thing in three interchangeable ways:

  1. Zoe feels humor
  2. Zoe's evaluation of a mistake and OK balance
  3. Zoe gains a new perspective (←humor described as its outcome)

There are many ways to describe humor as an action or outcome in different situations:

List of humor outcomes

  • Support an argument
  • Change negative emotions into positive ones
  • Create rapport
  • Ease tension
  • Give new perspectives
  • Help one become accepted
  • Reduce defensiveness
  • Reveal a personality trait
  • Reveal a personal view
  • Survive in high stress situations
  • Handle complaints
  • Deal with taboo topics
  • Open up a new topic of conversation
  • Acceptably criticize
  • Symbolize close ties
  • Control a situation
  • Achieve catharsis
  • Avoid conflict

These humor outcomes are the result of actions. This is why each humor outcome starts with a verb: we help someone become accepted, ease tension, support an argument, survive in high stress situations, and so on.

Since it’s easier to think about actions than it is to think about invented terminology (“mistakes and OKs”), we can use humor outcomes to think quickly and effectively about humor.

For instance:

When we say Zoe gained a new perspective, we’re talking about humor in a way that’s intuitive and narratively understandable. We know what this is like, and what sort of events do this, and why!

We feel humor when we gain a new perspective—which we looked at humor family words like insight, realization, revelation, and so on.

We'll be talking about humor outcomes in the rest of this chapter.

Actions and states. Humor outcomes and humor family words are similar in that both are situation-specific replacements for the generic word “humor.”

But they are different in that humor family words are states (achievement, insight, reconciliation, safety, and so on) while humor outcomes are actions (control a situation, avoid conflict, and so on).

Together, humor outcomes and humor family words provide two different ways of thinking about the same thing: humor.
