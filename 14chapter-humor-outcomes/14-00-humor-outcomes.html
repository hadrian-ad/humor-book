14. Humor Outcomes

In this chapter, weâ€™ll look at humor outcomes. These are ways of re-describing "balancing mistakes and OKs" as actions or outcomes.

For instance, we can call some situations where our mistakes and OKs balance as "gain a new perspective." This means gain a new perspective is a humor outcome:

Zoe is browsing the bookstore when she picks up a book on meditation. She reads the book title: Wherever You Go, There You Are.

She thinks about that for a second, puzzled. Then something clicks, and she gives an amused nose-exhale. (←humor↔︎a mistake is OK↔︎Zoe gained a new perspective)

Above, we're describing the same thing in three interchangeable ways:

  1. Zoe feels humor
  2. Zoe's evaluation of a mistake and OK balance
  3. Zoe gains a new perspective (←humor described as its outcome)

The humor outcome bundles more situational context and narrative clarity. This gives us a narratively intuitive way of talking and thinking about humor.

At first, it can seem odd to say gain a new perspective is the same thing as humor. But we've already seen how they are the same—we looked at how humor family words like insight, realization, revelation, and so on are humor.

When we say Zoe gained a new perspective, we're talking about insight, realization, revelation, and so on by other means—for the same reason these humor family words are humor, gaining a new perspective is humor.

We'll be talking about humor outcomes in the rest of this chapter.
