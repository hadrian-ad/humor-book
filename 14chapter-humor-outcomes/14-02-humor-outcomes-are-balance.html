Humor outcomes describe when mistakes and OKs balance

A humor outcome is the same thing as humor:

Zoe is browsing the bookstore when she picks up a book on meditation. She reads the book title: Wherever You Go, There You Are.

She thinks about that for a second, puzzled. Then something clicks, and she gives an amused nose-exhale. (←humor↔︎mistake and OK are balanced↔︎Zoe gains a new perspective)

Above, we're describing the same thing in three interchangeable ways:

  • Zoe feels humor
  • Zoe's evaluates a mistake and OK as balancing
  • Zoe gains a new perspective (←humor described as its outcome)

We could use any of the descriptions above to talk about Zoe's humor. Since the humor outcome (gain a new perspective) is the most narrative, it can be an intuitive way to predict and describe humor.

consider:

A title can't itself have a new perspective—it’s only a new perspective to someone. This is why we have to tether our humor outcomes to Zoe, not just to the title by itself. Zoe gains a new perspective.

Sameness. In this book, a humor outcome is humor. At first, this can seem odd—like how it's odd to say gain a new perspetive is humor. But we've already seen how these are the same.

In our chapter on humor family words, we looked at how words like insight, realization, revelation, and so on are humor. When Zoe gain a new perspective, she's feeling insight, realization, revelation, and so on—she's feeling humor.
