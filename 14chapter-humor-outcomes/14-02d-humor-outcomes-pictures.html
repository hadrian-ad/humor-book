The Humor Book

Humor outcomes add context to the word humor

We can use one humor outcome to describe the presence or absence of humor, but we can also use many:

Emma sees Minh down the hall. Uh-oh. She owes him coffee money, and he’s been hurt she hasn’t repaid him yet.

Before he sees her, she dodges down an alternate route. Phew. Safe for another day! She laughs at herself. (←Emma avoids conflict)

Above, instead of saying Emma only avoids conflict (by postponing it), we can also say she reaches a few different humor outcomes—she handled complaints (by avoiding them), eased (her own) tension, controlled a situation, and so on.

Sam and Jennifer are taking Riley to the kid-friendly farm. A sign at the goat enclosure says “GOATS HAVE NO MANNERS.”

Sam and Jennifer laugh. (←Sam and Jennifer feel the sign supports an argument)

Riley: (demanding) “What’s so funny?” (←Riley does not feel the sign supports an argument)

Above, Sam and Jennifer feel the sign supports an argument (about how to behave around goats), while Riley doesn't.

But while we can use one humor outcome to describe the presence or absence of humor, we can also use many humor outcomes to describe the presence or absence of humor:

Jennifer and Sam didn't feel the sign only support an argument—it also creates rapport, (makes Sam and Jennifer) feel accepted, deals with stress, (pre-emptively) handles complaints, opens a new topic of conversation, acceptably criticizes goats and attendees, symbolizes close ties (to goats and customers), controls a situation, avoids conflict (when giving a warning), and so on.

Consider:

We can use as many humor outcomes as we think are useful. Often, considering several humor outcomes help us see emotional situations in new light.

Overlap. Our humor outcomes all overlap with each other, like how creating rapport ovelaps with easing tension. These humor outcomes all overlap because they’re all re-describing the same thing: humor!

We can use as many humor outcomes as we think are useful. Often, using one feels precise enough. Other times, considering several humor outcomes help us see emotional situations in new light.
