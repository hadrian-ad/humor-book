The Humor Book

We'll use a specific list of humor outcomes

We'll use a specific list of humor outcomes in this book:

  • Support an argument
  • Change negative emotions into positive ones
  • Create rapport
  • Ease tension
  • Give new perspectives
  • Help one become accepted
  • Reduce defensiveness
  • Reveal a personality trait
  • Reveal a personal view
  • Survive in high stress situations
  • Handle complaints
  • Deal with taboo topics
  • Open up a new topic of conversation
  • Acceptably criticize
  • Symbolize close ties
  • Control a situation
  • Achieve catharsis
  • Avoid conflict

These humor outcomes are the result of actions, which is why each one centers around a verb: we help someone become accepted, ease tension, support an argument, survive in high stress situations, and so on.

Actions and states. Before, we looked at humor family words, which were emotions of humor—like achievement, insight, reconciliation, safety, and so on.

Now, we're looking at humor outcomes, which are actions of humor—avoid conflict, control a situation, gain a perspective, and so on.

Together, "humor family words" "humor outcomes" and provide two different ways of thinking and talking about the same thing: humor.
