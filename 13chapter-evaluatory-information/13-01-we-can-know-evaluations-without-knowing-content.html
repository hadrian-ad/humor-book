Units. We have units of distance (miles) and units of time (hours)—and while we can put them together (miles per hour), we can't treat them as the same thing. (1 mile + 1 hour ≠ 2 miles.) That's because time and distance are seperate kinds of things for day-to-day thinking.

We can think of "evaluatory information" and "content information" similarly—like two different kinds of things. Like miles per hour, these units are often used together—but just because they often go together doesn't mean there's only one thing there: there's actually two.
