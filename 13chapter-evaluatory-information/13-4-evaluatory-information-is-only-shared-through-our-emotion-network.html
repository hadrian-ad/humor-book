Evaluatory information is shared quickly

We exchange evaluatory information with lightning speed on our emotion network:

Justin brings Heather a gift. He watches her unwrap it. She opens the box and her face falls slightly, confused. (←Heather shares a lot of evaluatory information in a split second second)

Justin: (dispirited) "Don't like it?"

Heather: "No, I do. I'm just surprised."

Jessica is seating customers when she glances up. It's Elias! Her face bursts into a smile. (←Jessica shares a lot of evaluatory information in a split second second)

Sam comes home.

Sam: *closing door* "Man, what I day, I..."

He sees Jennifer, red-eyed at the table, and Riley sitting nervously on the couch. (←Sam, Jennifer, and Riley exchange a lot of evaluatory information in a single second)

Zoe and Emma are talking and walking down the hallway when Zoe looks through a window and sees Owen sitting in a classroom. They catch each other’s eye.

Zoe: *raises eyebrow*

Owen: (blearily) *smiles*

Zoe: (sympathetic) *smiles*

The window slides out of sight as Zoe and Emma continue walking, their conversation unbroken. (←Zoe and Owen exchange a lot of evaluatory information in a single second)

Emma, Zoe, Owen, Minh, and Trevor are in the library working on a group project for Justin’s class.

Zoe: "Okay." *reads assignment aloud* "‘Imagine Joseph Conrad and Robert Browning are teenagers in an American university and are debating the merits of 21st century Literary Modernism compared to 19th century Literary Exceptionalism. Work together to write a screenplay that includes their positions. Don’t forget: have fun!’” *pause* “Um.”

No one says anything for few seconds. (←everyone exchanges a lot of evaluatory information in a few seconds)

Emma: “Controversial take: let’s just use a chat bot and go get ice cream.”

Everyone reacts to the proposal. (←everyone exchanges a lot of evaluatory information in a few seconds)

Multiple people can survey each other's emotional state in just a few seconds. This is a huge amount of information, shared very quickly!

To see how efficient and dense this exchange of information is, we can compare sitauations—like when Emma, Zoe, Owen, Minh, and Trevor are:

  • all in the same room and take two seconds to glance at each other (full emotion network)
  • are all in different rooms and take two seconds to try to text their reaction to each other (constrained emotion network)

In the first case, it would take two seconds for everyone to convey their own opinion, while also gathering the nuanced stance of everyone involved.

In the second case, it would take two seconds to barely type two words, let alone also learn much about everyone else's emotional state.

A full emotion network, where we have access to all of someone's emotion tells, shares evaulatory information very quickly.

Constraint. An emotion network is accessed with degrees of fullness—that is, what proportion of emotion tells we have access to. So if we're talking to someone, we have access to all their emotion tells (full emotion network), but if we're just looking at a static picture, we don't have full access (constrained emotion network).

The more constrained an emotion network is, the less evaluatory information it has. So being around an angry man has the most evaluatory information—seeing a picture of him has less—and just reading a snippet of an article ("he was incensed") has the least evaluatory information.
