Sometimes, we think that the fittedness and intensity of humor are related in some way—like that if one goes up, the other must go down.

But fittedness and intensity aren’t connected—they’re separate measures. Below, we can see that one of Sam's anecdotes was both more fitted and generated more intense humor:

One variation was better by both measures.

That's because fittedness and intensity are independent measures, and we can’t correlate between them. We can't find one from the other—instead, we have to look for both fittedness and intensity.
