Dependence. Sometimes, we think that the fittedness and intensity of humor are related in some way—like that if one goes up, the other must go down.

But fittedness and intensity aren’t connected—they’re separate measures. Below, we can see that one of Sam's anecdotes was both more fitted and generated more intense humor:

One variation was better by both measures.

That's because fittedness and intensity are independent measures, and we can’t correlate between them. Since we can't find one from the other, we have to look for both fittedness and intensity.

Emotional actions and intentions have degrees of comparative fit to their emotional situation. We can estimate this "fittedness." Fittedness is a measure of appropriateness to situation.

  • We can estimate fittedness of humor or humor attempts by accounting for relevancy of topics, humor family words, and humor outcomes.
  • We can use these elements of fittedness to develop methodology for making fitted humor attempts. Higher fittedness tends to lead to greater toleration and breader emotional activity on an emotion network.
  • Fittedness and intensity are two different quantitative measures in our model of humor. They are independent variables.
